  technique_improves_learning : TechniqueImproved → LearnFaster
  reg_daily : PracticeRegularly → PracticeDaily

We are to prove: ¬LearnFaster → ListensAttentively

Let's break down:
  We assume ¬LearnFaster (i.e., Sarah cannot learn songs faster).
  We need to show that she listens attentively.

From the axioms, we can see:
  If she practices regularly, then by reg_daily she practices daily.
  Then by practice_improves_technique, her technique improves.
  Then by technique_improves_learning, she learns faster.
  But we have ¬LearnFaster, so if she practiced regularly, we would have a contradiction.

Therefore, she cannot have practiced regularly.

But we know from guitar_progress that either she practices regularly or she listens attentively (or both).

Since we have ruled out that she practices regularly, then she must listen attentively.

However, note that the disjunction is inclusive (or both). But if she does both, then we still have the regular practice leading to a contradiction? Actually, no: if she does both, then we have PracticeRegularly, which leads to LearnFaster, contradicting ¬LearnFaster. So in fact, she cannot have PracticeRegularly at all.

Therefore, the disjunction forces that she must have ListensAttentively.

So the proof is:

  Assume ¬LearnFaster.
  We want to show ListensAttentively.
  From guitar_progress, we have two cases: PracticeRegularly or ListensAttentively.
  In the first case (PracticeRegularly):
      reg_daily gives PracticeDaily.
      Then practice_improves_technique gives TechniqueImproved.
      Then technique_improves_learning gives LearnFaster.
      But we have ¬LearnFaster, so contradiction.
  Therefore, the first case is impossible, so we must have ListensAttentively.

In Lean, we can do:

  theorem conclusion : ¬LearnFaster → ListensAttentively :=
  fun h : ¬LearnFaster =>   -- h is the assumption that Sarah does not learn faster
  match guitar_progress with
  | Or.inl reg => 
      have daily : PracticeDaily := reg_daily reg
      have tech : TechniqueImproved := practice_improves_technique daily
      have lf : LearnFaster := technique_improves_learning tech
      False.elim (h lf)      -- contradiction
  | Or.inr listen => listen   -- in this case we directly have ListensAttentively

Alternatively, we can use `by` tactics:
