The context provides information about employees, promotions, influence, and motivation. We need to determine if we can prove that Mark is not motivated given that he impresses his bosses.

**Formalization:**
- `Employee` is the type of employees.
- `Mark` is a specific employee.
- Predicates:
  - `WorksHard e`: Employee e works hard.
  - `GetsPromotions e`: Employee e gets promotions.
  - `ImpressesBosses e`: Employee e impresses their bosses.
  - `GainsInfluence e`: Employee e gains influence.
  - `Motivated e`: Employee e is motivated.
- Axioms:
  1. Hard work leads to promotions: `∀ e, WorksHard e → GetsPromotions e`
  2. Impressing bosses leads to influence: `∀ e, ImpressesBosses e → GainsInfluence e`
  3. Not all employees succeeded: `∃ e, ¬GetsPromotions e ∧ ¬GainsInfluence e`
  4. Motivation leads to hard work: `∀ e, Motivated e → WorksHard e`
- Given: Mark impresses his bosses (`ImpressesBosses Mark`).

**Proof Attempt:**
We try to prove `¬Motivated Mark` (Mark is not motivated):
1. From `ImpressesBosses Mark` and axiom 2, we get `GainsInfluence Mark`.
2. If Mark were motivated (assume `Motivated Mark`), then by axiom 4, he works hard (`WorksHard Mark`), and by axiom 1, he gets promotions (`GetsPromotions Mark`).
3. Thus, Mark has both promotions and influence.
4. Axiom 3 states there exists some employee without promotions and influence, but this doesn't conflict with Mark's success (it could be a different employee).
5. No axioms link "impressing bosses" to "not being motivated" directly.
6. Therefore, we **cannot prove** `¬Motivated Mark`.

axiom Employee : Type
axiom Mark : Employee
axiom WorksHard : Employee → Prop
axiom GetsPromotions : Employee → Prop
axiom ImpressesBosses : Employee → Prop
axiom GainsInfluence : Employee → Prop
axiom Motivated : Employee → Prop

axiom A1 : ∀ e : Employee, WorksHard e → GetsPromotions e
axiom A2 : ∀ e : Employee, ImpressesBosses e → GainsInfluence e
axiom A3 : ∃ e : Employee, ¬GetsPromotions e ∧ ¬GainsInfluence e
axiom A4 : ∀ e : Employee, Motivated e → WorksHard e

axiom Mark_impresses : ImpressesBosses Mark

-- Prove GainsInfluence for Mark
theorem Mark_influence : GainsInfluence Mark := by
  apply A2 Mark Mark_impresses
