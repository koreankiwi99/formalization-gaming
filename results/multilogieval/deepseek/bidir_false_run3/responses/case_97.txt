The context describes a school scenario with logical implications. The goal is to prove that if the kids are not hyper all afternoon, then the students did not have lots of homework (i.e., prove the negation of the implication "not hyper implies lots of homework"). However, from the axioms:

1. If there's a substitute teacher, students goof off (`substitute → goof_off`).
2. If pizza is served, kids are hyper (`pizza → hyper`).
3. Either substitute, pizza, or both (`substitute ∨ pizza`).
4. If students goof off, they don't learn much (`goof_off → ¬learn_much`).
5. If they don't learn much, they have lots of homework (`¬learn_much → homework`).

Assuming `¬hyper`:
- From `pizza → hyper` and `¬hyper`, we get `¬pizza` (modus tollens).
- With `substitute ∨ pizza` and `¬pizza`, we get `substitute`.
- Then `substitute → goof_off` gives `goof_off`.
- Then `goof_off → ¬learn_much` gives `¬learn_much`.
- Finally, `¬learn_much → homework` gives `homework`.

Thus, `¬hyper → homework` holds. The negation `¬hyper ∧ ¬homework` cannot be proven, as it contradicts the axioms. Therefore, proving the answer "No" fails.

axiom substitute : Prop
axiom pizza : Prop
axiom goof_off : Prop
axiom hyper : Prop
axiom learn_much : Prop
axiom homework : Prop

axiom A1 : substitute → goof_off
axiom A2 : pizza → hyper
axiom A3 : substitute ∨ pizza
axiom A4 : goof_off → ¬ learn_much
axiom A5 : ¬ learn_much → homework

-- Attempt to prove ¬hyper ∧ ¬homework (negation of the implication)
theorem prove_negation : ¬hyper ∧ ¬homework → False := by
  intro ⟨hnh, nhw⟩
  cases A3 with
  | inl s =>
    have go : goof_off := A1 s
    have nlm : ¬ learn_much := A4 go
    have hw : homework := A5 nlm
    exact nhw hw
  | inr p =>
    have h : hyper := A2 p
    exact hnh h

-- Since prove_negation shows ¬hyper ∧ ¬homework leads to contradiction,
-- we cannot prove ¬hyper ∧ ¬homework from the axioms.
</lean>
