The reasoning involves Matt not redoing his living room and whether he gets new decorating ideas. The context provides several logical connections:

1. Watching DIY videos leads to gaining decorating skills.
2. Gaining decorating skills enables redoing the living room.
3. Matt did not redo his living room (given in the question).
4. Therefore, Matt did not gain decorating skills (by modus tollens from 2 and 3).
5. Since gaining skills requires watching videos (from 1), Matt did not watch DIY videos (by modus tollens from 4).
6. Matt either watched DIY videos, read blogs, or both (disjunction).
7. Since Matt did not watch videos, he must have read blogs (from 5 and 6).
8. Reading blogs leads to new decorating ideas.
9. Therefore, Matt gets new decorating ideas.

This chain of reasoning is formalized in Lean and proven.

<lean>
axiom Person : Type
axiom Matt : Person

axiom WatchedVideos : Person → Prop
axiom ReadBlogs : Person → Prop
axiom GainSkills : Person → Prop
axiom RedoLivingRoom : Person → Prop
axiom NewIdeas : Person → Prop

axiom video_skills : ∀ p : Person, WatchedVideos p → GainSkills p
axiom skills_redo : ∀ p : Person, GainSkills p → RedoLivingRoom p
axiom choice : ∀ p : Person, WatchedVideos p ∨ ReadBlogs p
axiom blogs_ideas : ∀ p : Person, ReadBlogs p → NewIdeas p

axiom matt_not_redo : ¬ RedoLivingRoom Matt

theorem matt_ideas : NewIdeas Matt := by
  -- Matt didn't gain skills (because skills would lead to redoing)
  have no_skills : ¬ GainSkills Matt := fun h => matt_not_redo (skills_redo Matt h)
  -- Thus, Matt didn't watch videos (since videos give skills)
  have no_videos : ¬ WatchedVideos Matt := fun h => no_skills (video_skills Matt h)
  -- By the choice axiom, Matt must have read blogs
  have read_blogs : ReadBlogs Matt := by
    cases (choice Matt) with
    | inl h_videos => contradiction
    | inr h_blogs => exact h_blogs
  -- Therefore, Matt gets new ideas from reading blogs
  exact blogs_ideas Matt read_blogs
</lean>

ANSWER: Yes
