The reasoning involves determining if the farmer makes a good profit given that pests invaded the field. The context provides several implications and a disjunction:

1. Enough water implies crops grow well.
2. Pests invasion implies crops destroyed.
3. The disjunction: enough water OR no crops destroyed OR (enough water AND crops destroyed). Logically, this simplifies to enough water OR no crops destroyed, as the third case is subsumed by the first.
4. Crops grow well implies bountiful harvest.
5. Bountiful harvest implies good profit.

Given pests invasion:
- Crops are destroyed (from 2).
- From the disjunction (3), since crops are destroyed, "no crops destroyed" is false, so enough water must be true.
- Enough water implies crops grow well (from 1).
- Crops grow well implies bountiful harvest (from 4).
- Bountiful harvest implies good profit (from 5).

Thus, pests invasion leads to good profit. The Lean proof confirms this.

<lean>
axiom enough_water : Prop
axiom crops_destroyed : Prop
axiom crops_grow_well : Prop
axiom harvest_bountiful : Prop
axiom good_profit : Prop
axiom pests_invade : Prop

axiom C1 : enough_water → crops_grow_well
axiom C2 : pests_invade → crops_destroyed
axiom C3 : enough_water ∨ ¬ crops_destroyed
axiom C4 : crops_grow_well → harvest_bountiful
axiom C5 : harvest_bountiful → good_profit

theorem answer : pests_invade → good_profit :=
  fun h : pests_invade =>
    have cd : crops_destroyed := C2 h
    have ew : enough_water :=
      Or.elim C3
        (fun h_ew : enough_water => h_ew)
        (fun h_no_cd : ¬ crops_destroyed => False.elim (h_no_cd cd))
    have cg : crops_grow_well := C1 ew
    have hb : harvest_bountiful := C4 cg
    show good_profit from C5 hb
</lean>

ANSWER: Yes
