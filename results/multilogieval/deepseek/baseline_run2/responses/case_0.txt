The reasoning follows from the given axioms:

1. If they save aggressively, they can afford a down payment (A1).
2. If they improve their credit, they qualify for a mortgage (A2).
3. They must either save aggressively or improve their credit (A3).
4. If they qualify for a mortgage, they buy their dream home (A4).
5. They cannot afford a down payment (T1).

Since they cannot afford a down payment (T1), they do not save aggressively (by A1). Then, by A3, they must improve their credit. By A2, improving their credit means they qualify for a mortgage. Therefore, by A4, they buy their dream home.

<lean>
axiom Person : Type
axiom p0 : Person
axiom saves_aggressively : Person → Prop
axiom afford_down_payment : Person → Prop
axiom improve_credit : Person → Prop
axiom qualify_mortgage : Person → Prop
axiom buy_dream_home : Person → Prop
axiom A1 : ∀ p, saves_aggressively p → afford_down_payment p
axiom A2 : ∀ p, improve_credit p → qualify_mortgage p
axiom A3 : ∀ p, saves_aggressively p ∨ improve_credit p
axiom A4 : ∀ p, qualify_mortgage p → buy_dream_home p
axiom T1 : ¬ afford_down_payment p0

theorem bought_home : buy_dream_home p0 := by
  -- They don't save aggressively since T1 says they don't afford down payment, and saving implies affording (A1)
  have no_save : ¬ saves_aggressively p0 := fun H_save => T1 (A1 p0 H_save)
  -- By A3, they must improve their credit
  have H_credit : improve_credit p0 := by
    cases A3 p0 with
    | inl H_save => exact False.elim (no_save H_save)
    | inr H_credit => exact H_credit
  -- By A2, improving credit qualifies them for a mortgage
  have H_qualify : qualify_mortgage p0 := A2 p0 H_credit
  -- By A4, qualifying means buying the dream home
  exact A4 p0 H_qualify

</lean>

ANSWER: Yes
