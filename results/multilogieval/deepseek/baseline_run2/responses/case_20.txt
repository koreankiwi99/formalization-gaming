The context provides general rules and observations about employees but does not establish a direct link between Mark's boss impression and his motivation. Specifically:

- We know Mark impresses his bosses (given), so he gains influence (Axiom 2).
- We know there exists an ambitious employee for whom motivation implies working hard (Axiom 4), but this might not be Mark.
- There are no axioms connecting motivation to impressing bosses, working hard to impressing bosses, or motivation to promotions/influence directly.
- The lack of universal promotions/influence (Axiom 3) implies some counterexamples but doesn't constrain Mark specifically.

Thus, Mark having influence does not force him to be motivated (he might be motivated or not), and the ambitious employee condition doesn't apply to Mark necessarily. Hence, neither `Motivated Mark` nor `¬Motivated Mark` is provable.
