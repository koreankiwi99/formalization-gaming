The context provides a conditional statement: if a company develops an innovative product and markets it effectively, then the product will be commercially successful. The given statements are that Acme Inc. marketed their gadget effectively, and the gadget was not commercially successful. We are to determine if it follows that Acme Inc. developed an innovative product.

Using modus tollens on the conditional rule:
- If (development of an innovative product ∧ effective marketing) → commercial success
- And we have ¬commercial success
- Then ¬(development of an innovative product ∧ effective marketing)

This simplifies to:
¬development of an innovative product ∨ ¬effective marketing

Since we know effective marketing occurred (for Acme and the gadget), it follows that Acme did NOT develop an innovative product. Therefore, we cannot prove that Acme Inc. developed an innovative product (the desired conclusion).
