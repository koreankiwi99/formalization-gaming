The context provides several logical relations regarding plant care, drought, water restrictions, and plant health. The key steps are:

1. If plants are watered daily (W), they grow well (G): W → G
2. Drought (D) causes water restrictions (R): D → R
3. Either daily watering or drought occurred (or both): W ∨ D
4. Water restrictions cause lack of water (L): R → L
5. Lack of water causes wilting (Wilt): L → Wilt

Given ¬G (plants didn't grow well), we prove Wilt (wilted and died):
- ¬G and W → G imply ¬W (contraposition)
- ¬W and W ∨ D imply D
- D and D → R imply R
- R and R → L imply L
- L and L → Wilt imply Wilt

Thus, "Priya's plants wilted and died" follows necessarily.
