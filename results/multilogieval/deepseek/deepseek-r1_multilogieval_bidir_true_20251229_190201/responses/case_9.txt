The context describes a party scenario with specific conditions. The key elements are:
- Bringing a dish implies enough food: `BroughtDish → EnoughFood`
- Birthday party implies cake and ice cream: `IsBirthdayParty → (Cake ∧ IceCream)`
- Either someone brought a dish or there was no cake: `BroughtDish ∨ ¬Cake`
- It is Sam's birthday party: `IsBirthdayParty`

From `IsBirthdayParty`, we deduce `Cake ∧ IceCream` (hence `Cake`). With `Cake` true, the disjunction `BroughtDish ∨ ¬Cake` forces `BroughtDish` (since `¬Cake` is false). Then `BroughtDish → EnoughFood` gives `EnoughFood`.

Lean formalization and proof:

<lean>
axiom IsBirthdayParty : Prop
axiom BroughtDish : Prop
axiom EnoughFood : Prop
axiom Cake : Prop
axiom IceCream : Prop

axiom rule_food : BroughtDish → EnoughFood
axiom rule_bday : IsBirthdayParty → (Cake ∧ IceCream)
axiom disjunction : BroughtDish ∨ ¬Cake

axiom sam_party : IsBirthdayParty

theorem sam_enough_food : EnoughFood := by
  have cake_ice : Cake ∧ IceCream := rule_bday sam_party
  have cake : Cake := cake_ice.left
  cases disjunction with
  | inl h => exact rule_food h
  | inr no_cake => contradiction
</lean>
