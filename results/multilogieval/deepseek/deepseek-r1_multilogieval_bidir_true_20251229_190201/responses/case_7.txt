The context provides the necessary premises to conclude that the harvest was abundant. The gardener enriched the soil, and after this action, the plants grew strong (indicating they were healthy) and produced lots of vegetables. Additionally, a general rule states that healthy plants yield bigger harvests. Since the plants were observed to be healthy, this directly implies an abundant harvest via the rule.

Formalized in Lean:
- `Garden` represents the domain.
- `g` is the specific garden in question.
- `soilEnriched g` indicates the soil was enriched.
- `plantsHealthy g` indicates the plants were healthy.
- `harvestAbundant g` indicates an abundant harvest.
- The rule `∀ x, plantsHealthy x → harvestAbundant x` maps health to abundance.

Premises:
1. `soilEnriched g` (soil was enriched by gardener's actions).
2. `plantsHealthy g` (plants grew strong).
3. The rule linking health to abundant harvests.

The theorem `harvest_abundant` is proven by applying the rule to the observed plant health.

<lean>
axiom Garden : Type
axiom g : Garden
axiom soilEnriched : Garden → Prop
axiom plantsHealthy : Garden → Prop
axiom harvestAbundant : Garden → Prop

axiom enriched : soilEnriched g
axiom healthy : plantsHealthy g
axiom rule : ∀ x : Garden, plantsHealthy x → harvestAbundant x

theorem harvest_abundant : harvestAbundant g := rule g healthy
</lean>
