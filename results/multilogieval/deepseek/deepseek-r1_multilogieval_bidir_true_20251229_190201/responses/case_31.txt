The context establishes two principles:  
1. If someone suspects cheating, they should have an open conversation.  
2. Infidelity concerns warrant re-evaluating the relationship.  

The statement about Priya is:  
¬ (talked to partner) ∨ ¬ (reassessed relationship)  

We need to determine if Priya suspected cheating.  

In Lean:  
- We formalize "suspects cheating" as `SuspectsCheating`.  
- "Infidelity concerns" as `InfidelityConcerns` (with suspicion implying concerns).  
- Normative actions: `ShouldHaveOpenConvo` and `ShouldReevaluate`.  
- Actual actions: `TalkedToPartner` and `Reassessed`.  

The gap:  
- The context only states what Priya *should* do if suspicions exist.  
- Her actual actions (or lack thereof) don't prove whether she had suspicions, as she might:  
  - Suspect but fail to act.  
  - Not suspect, making inaction irrelevant.  
- No axioms link normative obligations to actual behavior.  

Since we cannot derive `SuspectsCheating Priya` from the disjunction about her actions and the given norms, the answer is **Failure**.
