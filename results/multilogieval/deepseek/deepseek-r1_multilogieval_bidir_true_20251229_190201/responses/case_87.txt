2. If it's a coworker's birthday, people bring treats.
3. Either projects get done early, or it's a birthday, or both.
4. Taking a long lunch leads to feeling recharged.
5. Feeling recharged leads to a productive afternoon.

The question asks: If John didn't bring treats, did he have a productive afternoon?

- The "people bring treats" rule doesn't specify that every individual must bring treats on birthdays. John's absence in treat-bringing doesn't imply it wasn't a birthday (others may have brought treats).
- The disjunction in (3) might result in only others - not John - finishing projects early. Thus:
   - John may not have taken a long lunch (blocking the chain to productivity).
   - Birthday occurrence would not affect John's actions under given rules.
