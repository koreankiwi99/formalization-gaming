Failure. The Lean proof demonstrates that from the premises:
1. If marketing (M) occurred, sales (S) would increase
2. Either M or production costs (P) (or both) occurred
3. Sales did not increase (¬S)

We can logically deduce:
- ¬M (via modus tollens)
- Therefore P (from ¬M and M ∨ P)

Since we proved P (production costs increased), the negation ¬P cannot hold. The attempt to prove the negation of the conclusion failed.
