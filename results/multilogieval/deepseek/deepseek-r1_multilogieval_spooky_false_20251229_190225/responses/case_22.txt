To determine if the city is undergoing a drought, we analyze the premises and the given statement using first-order logic. The premises state that drought necessitates both water restrictions and water rationing. The disjunctive statement indicates that at least one of these measures is not implemented. By contraposition, this implies the absence of drought.

<lean>
axiom City : Type
axiom city : City
axiom drought : City → Prop
axiom implement_restrictions : City → Prop
axiom ration_water : City → Prop

-- Premise: Drought requires water restrictions
axiom A1 : ∀ (c : City), drought c → implement_restrictions c
-- Premise: Drought requires water rationing
axiom A2 : ∀ (c : City), drought c → ration_water c

-- Given statement: Either restrictions were not implemented or water was not rationed
axiom H : ¬implement_restrictions city ∨ ¬ration_water city

-- Proving the city is NOT in drought
theorem not_drought : ¬drought city := by
  intro h_drought  -- Assume drought exists for contradiction
  -- Derive both required measures from drought assumptions
  have restrictions : implement_restrictions city := A1 city h_drought
  have rationing : ration_water city := A2 city h_drought
  -- Case analysis on the disjunction H
  cases H with
  | inl no_restrictions => 
    apply no_restrictions restrictions  -- Contradiction from missing restrictions
  | inr no_rationing => 
    apply no_rationing rationing        -- Contradiction from missing rationing
</lean>

ANSWER: No
