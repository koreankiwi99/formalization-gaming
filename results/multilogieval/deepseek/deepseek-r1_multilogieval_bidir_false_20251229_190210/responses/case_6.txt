The query involves determining if flooding occurs in Dallas given heavy rainfall and general rules about rainfall causing overflow and flooding. The key issue is the lack of a direct link between heavy rainfall and excessive rain in the premises.

**Formalization in Lean:**
- `City` is the type of cities, with `Dallas` as a specific city.
- Predicates:
  - `HeavyRain c`: city `c` has heavy rainfall.
  - `ExcessiveRain c`: city `c` has excessive rain (required for overflow).
  - `Overflow c`: reservoirs overflow in city `c`.
  - `Flooding c`: flooding occurs in city `c`.
- Axioms:
  - `R1`: Excessive rain causes overflow: `∀ c, ExcessiveRain c → Overflow c`
  - `R2`: Overflow causes flooding: `∀ c, Overflow c → Flooding c`
  - `T1`: Dallas has heavy rainfall: `HeavyRain Dallas`

**Proof Attempt:**
The goal is to prove **`¬Flooding Dallas`** (the negation of the conclusion that flooding occurs). However:
1. No axiom connects `HeavyRain` to `ExcessiveRain`, so we cannot infer that Dallas had excessive rain.
2. Without `ExcessiveRain Dallas`, we cannot use `R1` to derive `Overflow Dallas`.
3. Without `Overflow Dallas`, we cannot use `R2` to derive `Flooding Dallas`—nor can we prove its negation.

Since there is insufficient information to prove that flooding **does not** occur (`¬Flooding Dallas`), the proof attempt fails.
