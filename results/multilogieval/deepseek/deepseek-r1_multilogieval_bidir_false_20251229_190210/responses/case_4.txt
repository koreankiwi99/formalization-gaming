The context states that if a company invests in marketing, sales increase. Additionally, either marketing was focused on, production costs increased, or both. Given that sales did not increase, the goal is to prove that production costs did not increase (¬C).

However, from the premises:
1. Marketing investment leads to sales increase (M → S)
2. Sales did not increase (¬S)
3. Either marketing or cost increase occurred (M ∨ C)

We deduce:
- From ¬S and M → S, we get ¬M (contraposition).
- From ¬M and M ∨ C, we get C (disjunctive syllogism).

Thus, we can prove that production costs increased (C). This means we cannot prove the negation (¬C). Hence, the proof attempt fails.

<lean>
axiom M : Prop -- invested in marketing
axiom C : Prop -- production costs increased
axiom S : Prop -- sales increased

axiom marketing_leads_to_sales : M → S
axiom marketing_or_costs : M ∨ C
axiom sales_not_increase : ¬S

-- Prove production costs increased (C)
example : C := by
  have notM : ¬M := fun m => sales_not_increase (marketing_leads_to_sales m)
  cases marketing_or_costs
  · contradiction
  · assumption

-- Attempt to prove ¬C fails as it contradicts the above result
</lean>
