The context provides logical relationships between practicing chords, developing muscle memory, playing songs, focusing on music theory, and gaining knowledge about song structure. The statement to evaluate is whether Amy must have knowledge about song structure if she cannot play any songs.

In Lean:
- Define a type `obj` for individuals and constants for predicates.
- Axioms capture:
  1. Practicing chords daily (`P`) leads to muscle memory (`M`).
  2. Muscle memory enables playing simple songs (`S`).
  3. Everyone either practices chords daily or focuses on music theory (`T`) (or both).
  4. Focusing on music theory leads to knowledge about song structure (`K`).
- Given that Amy cannot play songs (`¬S Amy`), we attempt to prove she lacks knowledge (`¬K Amy`).

However, from the premises:
- `¬S Amy` implies `¬M Amy` (from axiom `A2` by contraposition).
- `¬M Amy` implies `¬P Amy` (from axiom `A1` by contraposition).
- `¬P Amy` and the disjunction `P Amy ∨ T Amy` (from `A3`) force `T Amy`.
- `T Amy` implies `K Amy` (from `A4`).

Thus, we prove `K Amy` (Amy has knowledge), not `¬K Amy`. We cannot prove the negation.

axiom S : obj → Prop
axiom T : obj → Prop

axiom A2 : ∀ x, M x → S x
