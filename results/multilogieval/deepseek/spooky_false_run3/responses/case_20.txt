The context provides premises about employees, promotions, influence, and motivation. The goal is to determine if Mark is motivated, given that he impresses his bosses. After formalizing the context in Lean, an attempt is made to prove that Mark is not motivated. However, the premises do not provide a direct link between Mark's motivation and his actions or outcomes. Specifically:

- Mark impresses his bosses, so he gains influence (by `P2`).
- There exists an employee who lacks both promotion and influence (by `P3`), but this does not involve Mark.
- An ambitious employee exists for whom motivation implies hard work (by `P4`), but Mark's ambition is not established.

No premise connects Mark's motivation to his influence, promotions, or hard work. Thus, the negation (Mark is not motivated) cannot be proven from the premises.

axiom P3 : ∃ x, ¬Promotion x ∧ ¬Influence x
-- There exists an ambitious employee such that if motivated, they work hard.
axiom P4 : ∃ a, Ambitious a ∧ (Motivated a → WorksHard a)
-- Mark impresses his bosses.
axiom Mark_impresses : ImpressesBosses Mark

-- Attempt to prove Mark is not motivated.

  rcases P4 with ⟨a, h_amb, h_mot_work⟩
