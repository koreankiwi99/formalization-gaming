The reasoning involves proving the negation of the statement: "If it was sunny out, then the team held their heads up high." This requires showing that it is sunny but the team did not hold their heads up high. However, the premises lead to a contradiction under this assumption:

1. If sunny, then children play at the park (P2).
2. Either the team practices daily or children do not play at the park (P3). If children play (from sunny), the team must practice daily.
3. If the team practices daily, they are prepared (P1).
4. If prepared, they are confident (P4).
5. If confident, they hold their heads up high (P5).

Thus, sunny implies the team holds their heads up high. Attempting to prove the negation (sunny and not holding heads high) leads to a contradiction, as shown in the Lean proof. Since the negation cannot be proven, the result is Failure.

axiom Sunny : Prop
axiom PlayAtPark : Prop
axiom PracticesDaily : Prop
axiom Prepared : Prop
axiom Confident : Prop
axiom HoldsHeadHigh : Prop

axiom P1 : PracticesDaily → Prepared
axiom P2 : Sunny → PlayAtPark
axiom P3 : PracticesDaily ∨ ¬PlayAtPark
axiom P4 : Prepared → Confident
axiom P5 : Confident → HoldsHeadHigh

theorem prove_negation : ¬(Sunny ∧ ¬HoldsHeadHigh) :=
  by
    intro h
    cases h with
    | intro hSunny notHigh =>
      have hPlay : PlayAtPark := P2 hSunny
      have hPractice : PracticesDaily := by
        cases P3 with
        | inl p => exact p
        | inr np => contradiction
      have hPrepared : Prepared := P1 hPractice
      have hConfident : Confident := P4 hPrepared
      have hHigh : HoldsHeadHigh := P5 hConfident
      contradiction
</lean>
