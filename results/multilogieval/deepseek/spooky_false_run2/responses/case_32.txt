The goal is to evaluate whether Jane values wellness based on the given context. The premises are:

1. If someone exercises regularly, they are fit: `∀ c, Exercises c → Fit c`
2. If someone eats nutritious foods, they have energy: `∀ c, EatsWell c → Energetic c`
3. Not everyone is active (fit) or energetic: `∃ c, ¬Fit c ∧ ¬Energetic c`
4. If someone values wellness, they exercise regularly: `∀ c, ValuesWellness c → Exercises c`
5. Jane eats nutritious foods: `EatsWell Jane`

We are to prove the negation: that Jane does **not** value wellness (i.e., `¬ValuesWellness Jane`).

### Attempted Proof in Lean:
- From premise 5 and premise 2, Jane has energy: `Energetic Jane`.
- Premise 3 provides a citizen `c0` who is not fit and not energetic: `¬Fit c0 ∧ ¬Energetic c0`.
- If Jane valued wellness (assume `ValuesWellness Jane`), she would exercise (by premise 4), leading to her being fit (by premise 1). Combined with her energy level, this gives `Fit Jane ∧ Energetic Jane`.
- However, `c0` and Jane are distinct entities (since Jane is energetic, and `c0` is not). The existence of `c0` does not restrict Jane's behavior, so no contradiction arises from Jane exercising and being fit/energetic.
- There is **no direct or derived premise** forcing Jane to be inactive or lacking energy. Without evidence that Jane fails to exercise, the assumption that she values wellness does not lead to a contradiction.
- Thus, the negation cannot be proven from the premises.

### Conclusion:
Failed to prove that Jane does not value wellness. The answer is **Failure**.

axiom Citizen : Type
axiom Jane : Citizen
axiom Exercises : Citizen → Prop
axiom EatsWell : Citizen → Prop
axiom Fit : Citizen → Prop

axiom ValuesWellness : Citizen → Prop

axiom values_exercise : ∀ (c : Citizen), ValuesWellness c → Exercises c
-- Premise 5: Jane eats well
axiom jane_eats_well : EatsWell Jane

-- Attempt to prove: Jane does NOT value wellness
theorem jane_not_values_wellness : ¬ ValuesWellness Jane :=
  -- Assume Jane values wellness
  fun h : ValuesWellness Jane =>

  -- Extract a citizen c0 who is not fit and not energetic

  have not_fit_c0 : ¬Fit c0 := hc0.1
  have not_energetic_c0 : ¬Energetic c0 := hc0.2
  -- Jane is fit and energetic, but c0 is distinct. This is not a contradiction.
  -- We have no information showing Jane doesn't exercise, so the assumption stands without contradiction.
  -- Failed to derive False; proof cannot be completed.
  sorry -- Indicates unprovable negation

ANSWER: Failure
