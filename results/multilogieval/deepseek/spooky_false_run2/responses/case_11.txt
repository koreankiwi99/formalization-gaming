The premises establish the following logical relationships:  
1. If the display looks nice, judges are impressed.  
2. If the research is thorough, experts take the project seriously.  
3. At least one of the following is true: display looks nice or research is thorough.  
4. If experts take the project seriously, it receives an award.  

Given that the judges were not impressed, we infer the display was not nice. By premise 3, research must be thorough. Premise 2 then implies experts take the project seriously, and premise 4 ensures the project receives an award.  

Thus, the project received an award. There is no way to prove the negation (that it did not receive an award) from the premises, as they entail the opposite.
