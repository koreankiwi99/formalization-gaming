<lean>
axiom obj : Type
axiom FarmerJoe : obj
axiom PlantedProperly : obj → Prop
axiom AdequateSunAndWater : obj → Prop
axiom GoodGrowth : obj → Prop

-- Context axiom: General gardening principle
axiom GardeningPrinciple : ∀ (f : obj), PlantedProperly f → (AdequateSunAndWater f → GoodGrowth f)

-- Given facts about Farmer Joe
axiom JoeWatered : AdequateSunAndWater FarmerJoe
axiom JoeNoGoodGrowth : ¬ GoodGrowth FarmerJoe

-- To prove: Farmer Joe did NOT plant properly
theorem joe_not_planted_properly : ¬ PlantedProperly FarmerJoe :=
  fun (hPlant : PlantedProperly FarmerJoe) =>
    have hGrowth : GoodGrowth FarmerJoe := GardeningPrinciple FarmerJoe hPlant JoeWatered
    JoeNoGoodGrowth hGrowth
</lean>

ANSWER: No

Explanation:
- The gardening principle states: If seeds are planted properly, then adequate sunlight/water implies good growth (∀f, PlantedProperly f → (AdequateSunWater f → GoodGrowth f))
- We know Farmer Joe had adequate sun/water (JoeWatered) but did NOT have good growth (JoeNoGoodGrowth)
- By modus tollens: If Joe had planted properly, he must have had good growth (via the principle applied to his sun/water)
- Since he lacked good growth despite adequate sun/water, he must not have planted properly
- The Lean proof shows ¬PlantedProperly FarmerJoe by deriving a contradiction from the assumption that he planted properly
