The reasoning involves evaluating whether the grass and plants were watered naturally today, given that the weather was very hot. The premises are:

1. If it rains, the grass and plants are watered naturally: \( R \to W \).
2. If the weather is very hot, children play in sprinklers: \( H \to P \).
3. Either it rained today or the children did not play in sprinklers: \( R \lor \neg P \).

Given that the weather was very hot (\( H \)), we derive:
- From \( H \) and premise 2, children played in sprinklers (\( P \)).
- With \( P \) true, premise 3 (\( R \lor \neg P \)) simplifies to \( R \) (since \( \neg P \) is false).
- From \( R \) and premise 1, the grass and plants were watered naturally (\( W \)).

Thus, the conclusion follows.

axiom R : Prop  -- It rained today
axiom W : Prop  -- Grass and plants were watered naturally today
axiom H : Prop  -- Weather was very hot today
axiom P : Prop  -- Children played in sprinklers today

axiom P1 : R → W
axiom P2 : H → P
axiom P3 : R ∨ ¬P
axiom given_H : H

theorem watered : W := by
  have p : P := P2 given_H
  cases P3 with
  | inl r => exact P1 r
  | inr np => contradiction
