2. Premise 3 states: "Either someone brought a dish to share, or there was no birthday cake."
3. Since we have cake (from step 1), the second disjunct ("no birthday cake") is false. Therefore, someone must have brought a dish.
4. Premise 1 states: "If someone brings a dish to share, there will be enough food for everyone." Thus, with someone bringing a dish, there is enough food.

This chain of reasoning proves that Sam has enough food.

<lean>
axiom is_birthday_party : Prop
axiom someone_brought_dish : Prop
axiom enough_food : Prop
axiom cake : Prop

axiom P1 : someone_brought_dish → enough_food
axiom P2 : is_birthday_party → cake
axiom P3 : someone_brought_dish ∨ ¬ cake

axiom birthday : is_birthday_party

theorem sam_has_enough_food : enough_food := by
  have has_cake : cake := P2 birthday
  cases P3 with
  | inl brought => exact P1 brought
  | inr no_cake => contradiction
