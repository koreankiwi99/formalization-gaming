The natural language reasoning involves evaluating whether Acme Inc. developed an innovative product given the premises. The universal premise states that for any company and product, if the company develops an innovative product and markets it effectively, then the product will be commercially successful. The specific facts are: Acme marketed the gadget effectively, but the gadget was not successful. 

To prove the affirmative conclusion (that Acme developed an innovative product), we assume it holds and derive a contradiction. If Acme developed an innovative gadget, then by the premise, effective marketing would imply commercial success. However, Acme's gadget was unsuccessful despite effective marketing. This contradiction means the assumption must be false, so Acme **did not** develop an innovative product. 

This proves the **negation** of the conclusion. Since we are only to prove the **affirmative** ("Yes"), and we have instead proved its falsehood, the proof of the affirmative conclusion fails.
