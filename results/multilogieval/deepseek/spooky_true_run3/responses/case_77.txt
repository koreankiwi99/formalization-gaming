2. If someone is picked to start, they are talented: `∀ p, PickedStart p → Talented p`
3. Either someone practices hard or they are not talented: `∀ p, PracHard p ∨ ¬ Talented p`
4. If someone has natural ability, they get picked to start: `∀ p, NaturalAbility p → PickedStart p`
5. If someone was a top player in youth leagues, they have natural ability: `∀ p, TopPlayer p → NaturalAbility p`

- Premise 4 states natural ability implies picked to start. By contraposition, not picked to start implies no natural ability (`¬ NaturalAbility James`).
- Premise 5 states top player implies natural ability. By contraposition, no natural ability implies not a top player (`¬ TopPlayer James`).
- Thus, under the assumption James did not play well, we derive he is not a top player. This contradicts the consequent of the implication (he is a top player). Therefore, the implication `¬ PlayWell James → TopPlayer James` cannot be proven.
