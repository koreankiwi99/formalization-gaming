The context describes a bookstore scenario with logical premises. The owner believes that great customer service leads to reputation growth, and hosting author events increases customer traffic. Additionally, the owner either provides excellent service or hosts events. Increased customer traffic leads to improved revenue. The question asks whether revenue improved given that service quality decreased.

Formalization:
- `S`: Service quality is excellent (great customer service)
- `H`: Hosts author events
- `C`: Customer traffic increases (more customers visit)
- `V`: Revenue improves

Premises:
1. `S → reputation_growth` (but reputation growth is irrelevant to revenue, so omitted)
2. `H → C` (hosting events increases traffic)
3. `S ∨ H` (owner provides excellent service or hosts events)
4. `C → V` (increased traffic improves revenue)
5. `¬S` (given: service quality decreased)

Proof:
- From `¬S` and `S ∨ H`, we deduce `H` (disjunctive syllogism).
- From `H` and `H → C`, we get `C`.
- From `C` and `C → V`, we conclude `V`.

Thus, revenue improved.

axiom H : Prop
axiom C : Prop
axiom V : Prop
axiom P1 : H → C
axiom P2 : S ∨ H
axiom P3 : C → V
axiom given : ¬S

theorem revenue_improves : V := by
  have h_H : H := by
    cases P2 with
    | inl s => contradiction
    | inr h => exact h
  exact P3 (P1 h_H)
</lean>
