The reasoning involves proving that if the kids did not go for a swim, then the players had smiles on their faces. The premises are formalized as follows:

- `¬R → S`: If it doesn't rain, the softball game occurs.
- `P → K`: If the pool is open, kids go swimming.
- `¬R ∨ P`: It either doesn't rain or the pool is open (or both).
- `S → E`: If the game occurs, players enjoy playing.
- `E → M`: If players enjoy playing, they smile afterward.

Given `¬K` (kids didn't swim), we derive:
1. Contrapositive of `P → K` gives `¬K → ¬P` (pool not open).
2. With `¬P` and `¬R ∨ P`, disjunctive syllogism yields `¬R`.
3. `¬R` implies `S` (game occurs).
4. `S` implies `E` (players enjoy).
5. `E` implies `M` (smiles occur).

Thus, `¬K → M` holds.
