The premises establish a general rule: If one plants tomato seeds properly, then adequate sunlight and watering ensure that the tomatoes will grow large and plentiful. For Farmer Joe, we know:  
1. He provided adequate sunlight and watering (fact1).  
2. His tomatoes did not grow large and plentiful (fact2).  

We need to determine whether Farmer Joe planted the tomato seeds properly.  

In Lean, we model this as follows:  
- All predicates are defined per person.  
- The general rule states that for any person, proper planting implies that if conditions are adequate, growth occurs.  
- Applied to Farmer Joe: His tomatoes did not grow despite adequate conditions, so his planting must have been improper.  

The Lean proof shows that assuming Joe planted properly leads to a contradiction (because growth would occur). Thus, we prove he did **not** plant properly.  

<lean>
axiom Person : Type
axiom Joe : Person
axiom PlantedProperly : Person → Prop
axiom AdequateConditions : Person → Prop
axiom GrowPlentiful : Person → Prop

axiom rule : ∀ p : Person, PlantedProperly p → AdequateConditions p → GrowPlentiful p
axiom fact1 : AdequateConditions Joe
axiom fact2 : ¬ GrowPlentiful Joe

theorem joe_did_not_plant_properly : ¬ PlantedProperly Joe := by
  intro h  -- Assume Joe planted properly
  have grow : GrowPlentiful Joe := rule Joe h fact1
  exact fact2 grow
</lean>

The Lean proof demonstrates that the premises entail Joe did not plant properly. Thus, the answer to "Did Farmer Joe plant the tomato seeds properly?" is **No**, which we prove conclusively.
