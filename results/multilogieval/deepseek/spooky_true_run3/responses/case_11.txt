   - "If experts take seriously, project receives award": `∀p, ExpertsTakeSeriously p → ReceivesAward p`

2. **Given Fact**: Judges were not impressed for the student's project (`¬JudgesImpressed studentProject`).

   - If it had a nice display, premise 1 would imply judges are impressed, contradicting the given fact. Thus, it cannot have a nice display.
   - Therefore, it must have thorough research (by disjunctive syllogism).
   - By premise 2, thorough research implies experts take it seriously.
   - By premise 4, experts taking it seriously implies the project receives an award.

4. **Lean Proof**:
   - Uses case analysis on the disjunction (`DisplayNice studentProject ∨ ResearchThorough studentProject`).
   - The nice display case leads to contradiction with the given fact.
   - The thorough research case directly implies the award via premises 2 and 4.

axiom obj : Type

axiom ExpertsTakeSeriously : obj → Prop

axiom Premise3 : ∀ p : obj, DisplayNice p ∨ ResearchThorough p
