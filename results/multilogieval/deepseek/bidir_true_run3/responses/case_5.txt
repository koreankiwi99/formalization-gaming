The context provides logical premises about home owners and gardening. Mary is a specific home owner, and we know her plants did not thrive. The goal is to determine if she sought advice from experienced gardeners.

Premises:
1. If someone learned botany, they understand plant needs: `∀ p, LearnedBotany p → UnderstandNeeds p`
2. If someone understands plant needs, they grow thriving plants: `∀ p, UnderstandNeeds p → GrowThriving p`
3. For each person, they either learned botany, sought advice, or both: `∀ p, LearnedBotany p ∨ SoughtAdvice p`
4. Mary's plants did not thrive: `¬ GrowThriving Mary`

From premises 1 and 2, if Mary learned botany, her plants would thrive (by transitivity). Since they did not thrive, she did not learn botany. Premise 3 states she either learned botany or sought advice (or both). Since she did not learn botany, she must have sought advice. Thus, the conclusion follows.

<lean>
axiom Person : Type
axiom Mary : Person
axiom LearnedBotany : Person → Prop
axiom UnderstandNeeds : Person → Prop
axiom GrowThriving : Person → Prop
axiom SoughtAdvice : Person → Prop

axiom P1 : ∀ p : Person, LearnedBotany p → UnderstandNeeds p
axiom P2 : ∀ p : Person, UnderstandNeeds p → GrowThriving p
axiom P3 : ∀ p : Person, LearnedBotany p ∨ SoughtAdvice p
axiom Fact : ¬ GrowThriving Mary

theorem mary_sought_advice : SoughtAdvice Mary := by
  -- If Mary learned botany, her plants would thrive (from P1 and P2)
  have chain : LearnedBotany Mary → GrowThriving Mary := 
    fun h => P2 Mary (P1 Mary h)
  -- Since plants didn't thrive, she didn't learn botany
  have no_learn : ¬ LearnedBotany Mary := 
    fun h => Fact (chain h)
  -- From P3, she must have sought advice
  cases P3 Mary with
  | inl learned => contradiction
  | inr sought => exact sought
</lean>

ANSWER: Yes
