The context states that chefs know using fresh ingredients makes food delicious, cooking a complicated recipe takes more time, and the chef used fresh ingredients. The question asks if cooking a complicated recipe implies the food tasted delicious.

Since the chef used fresh ingredients (given), and fresh ingredients imply delicious food (premise), the food must taste delicious regardless of recipe complexity. Therefore, if the chef cooked a complicated recipe, the food still tastes delicious because freshness guarantees deliciousness.

<lean>
axiom UsesFresh : Prop
axiom TastesDelicious : Prop
axiom CooksComplicated : Prop
axiom TakesMoreTime : Prop

axiom A1 : UsesFresh → TastesDelicious
axiom A2 : CooksComplicated → TakesMoreTime
axiom A3 : UsesFresh

theorem complicated_implies_delicious : CooksComplicated → TastesDelicious :=
  fun _ => A1 A3
</lean>

ANSWER: Yes
