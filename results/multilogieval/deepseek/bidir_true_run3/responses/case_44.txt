The reasoning involves proving that Mark's garden failed to thrive given the premises and the fact that he did not need to care for the garden plants. The key steps are:

1. From "if someone plants a garden, they need to water and care" and "Mark did not need to care", we conclude Mark did not plant a garden (¬PlantsGarden).
2. From "either plant a garden or drought" and ¬PlantsGarden, we get Drought.
3. From "drought → watering ban" and Drought, we get BanOnWatering.
4. From "watering ban → not enough water" and BanOnWatering, we get NotEnoughWater.
5. From "not enough water → garden fails" and NotEnoughWater, we get GardenFails.

Thus, the garden failed to thrive is provable.

<lean>
axiom PlantsGarden : Prop
axiom Drought : Prop
axiom BanOnWatering : Prop
axiom NotEnoughWater : Prop
axiom GardenFails : Prop
axiom NeedsWaterAndCare : Prop

axiom A1 : PlantsGarden → NeedsWaterAndCare
axiom A2 : Drought → BanOnWatering
axiom A3 : PlantsGarden ∨ Drought
axiom A4 : BanOnWatering → NotEnoughWater
axiom A5 : NotEnoughWater → GardenFails
axiom not_needs : ¬ NeedsWaterAndCare

theorem garden_fails : GardenFails := by
  have not_plant : ¬ PlantsGarden := fun h => not_needs (A1 h)
  cases A3 with
  | inl plant => contradiction
  | inr drought =>
      have ban : BanOnWatering := A2 drought
      have no_water : NotEnoughWater := A4 ban
      exact A5 no_water
</lean>
