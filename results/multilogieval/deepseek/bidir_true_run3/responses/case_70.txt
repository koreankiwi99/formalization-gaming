The reasoning involves determining if Farmer Joe planted the tomato seeds properly based on the given context and facts. The context states that proper planting implies that adequate sunlight and watering lead to large and plentiful tomatoes. The facts are that Farmer Joe provided adequate sunlight and watering, but the tomatoes did not grow large and plentiful.

Formalizing in Lean:
- Let `P` be the proposition that Farmer Joe planted the tomato seeds properly.
- Let `S` be the proposition that there was adequate sunlight and watering (given as true).
- Let `G` be the proposition that the tomatoes grew large and plentiful (given as false).

The context is: `P → (S → G)`, which is equivalent to `(P ∧ S) → G`.
Given `S` is true and `G` is false, we can prove `¬P` using modus tollens.

<lean>
axiom P : Prop
axiom S : Prop
axiom G : Prop
axiom R1 : P → (S → G)
axiom T1 : S
axiom T2 : ¬G

theorem not_planted_properly : ¬P := by
  intro hP
  have h : G := R1 hP T1
  exact T2 h
</lean>

The Lean proof shows that assuming `P` leads to a contradiction (since `G` must hold but `¬G` is given). Therefore, `¬P` is proven, meaning Farmer Joe did not plant the tomato seeds properly.
