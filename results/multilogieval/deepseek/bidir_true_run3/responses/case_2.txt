The reasoning involves proving that the grass and plants were watered naturally given the premises and the fact that the weather was very hot.

1. **Premises**:
   - If it rains, the grass and plants are watered naturally: `Rained → Watered_naturally`.
   - If the weather is very hot, children play in sprinklers: `Hot → Play_in_sprinklers`.
   - Either it rained today or the children did not play in sprinklers: `Rained ∨ ¬Play_in_sprinklers`.
   - The weather was very hot: `Hot` (given).

2. **Proof**:
   - From `Hot` and the second premise, we derive `Play_in_sprinklers`.
   - The disjunction `Rained ∨ ¬Play_in_sprinklers` must hold. Since `Play_in_sprinklers` is true, `¬Play_in_sprinklers` is false. Thus, `Rained` must be true by disjunctive syllogism.
   - From `Rained` and the first premise, `Watered_naturally` follows.

<lean>
axiom Rained : Prop
axiom Watered_naturally : Prop
axiom Hot : Prop
axiom Play_in_sprinklers : Prop

axiom T1 : Rained → Watered_naturally
axiom T2 : Hot → Play_in_sprinklers
axiom T3 : Rained ∨ ¬ Play_in_sprinklers
axiom T4 : Hot

theorem watered : Watered_naturally := by
  have play : Play_in_sprinklers := T2 T4
  cases T3 with
  | inl rain => exact T1 rain
  | inr no_play => contradiction
</lean>

ANSWER: Yes
