The reasoning involves multiple premises and requires proving that if it was sunny, then the team held their heads up high. The premises are:
1. If the team practices every day, they are prepared (for the big game).
2. If it is sunny, children play at the park.
3. Either the team practiced daily or kids did not go to the park.
4. If the team feels prepared, they have confidence.
5. If people feel confident, they hold their heads up high.

Key challenges:
- The term "practices every day" in premise 1 suggests a habitual action, but premise 3 refers to a specific day ("today"). Without linking habitual practice to specific daily practice, we cannot derive preparedness from today's practice alone.
- Preparedness (premise 1) and feeling prepared (premise 4) are distinct concepts with no provided connection.
- Confidence (premise 4) is attributed to the team, while holding heads high (premise 5) applies to "people" (individuals), lacking a bridge from team confidence to individual actions.

Formalization in Lean:
- Propositions are modeled for the specific day ("today").
- `Practices` means the team practiced today (not necessarily habitually).
- No axiom connects objective preparedness (`Prepared`) to subjective feeling prepared (`FeelsPrepared`).
- No axiom links team confidence (`Confidence`) to individual actions (`HoldHeadHigh`).

Proof attempt:
- From `Sunny`, we derive `ChildrenPlay` (premise 2).
- Using `ChildrenPlay` and premise 3, we prove `Practices` (the team practiced today).
- With `Practices`, premise 1 gives `Prepared`.
- However, without `Prepared → FeelsPrepared`, we cannot get `Confidence` (premise 4) or `HoldHeadHigh` (premise 5).

Thus, the conclusion cannot be proven from the premises.

<lean>
axiom Sunny : Prop
axiom ChildrenPlay : Prop
axiom Practices : Prop
axiom Prepared : Prop
axiom FeelsPrepared : Prop
axiom Confidence : Prop
axiom HoldHeadHigh : Prop

axiom P1 : Practices → Prepared
axiom P2 : Sunny → ChildrenPlay
axiom P3 : Practices ∨ ¬ChildrenPlay
axiom P4 : FeelsPrepared → Confidence
axiom P5 : Confidence → HoldHeadHigh

theorem sunny_implies_practices (hS : Sunny) : Practices := by
  have hPlay := P2 hS
  cases P3 with
  | inl p => exact p
  | inr np => contradiction

theorem sunny_implies_prepared (hS : Sunny) : Prepared := 
  P1 (sunny_implies_practices hS)

-- Cannot prove FeelsPrepared from Prepared
-- Hence, cannot prove Sunny → HoldHeadHigh
</lean>
