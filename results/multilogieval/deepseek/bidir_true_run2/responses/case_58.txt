To solve the reasoning problem, we translate the natural language context into formal axioms and then prove whether the conclusion follows. Here, we model the scenario using propositional logic since entities are not explicitly named, only actions and outcomes.

**Predicates Definitions:**
- `add_salt`: Someone adds salt to the stew.
- `savory`: The stew becomes savory.
- `stir_continuously`: Someone stirs the stew continuously.
- `thickens_perfectly`: The stew thickens perfectly.
- `taste_stew`: Someone tastes the stew.
- `prepare_salad`: Someone prepares a salad.

**Premises:**
1. If someone adds salt, the stew becomes savory: `add_salt ‚Üí savory`.
2. If someone stirs continuously, the stew thickens perfectly: `stir_continuously ‚Üí thickens_perfectly`.
3. Either someone adds salt or the stew doesn't thicken perfectly (inclusive "or"): `add_salt ‚à® ¬¨thickens_perfectly`.
4. If someone tastes the stew, they stir it continuously: `taste_stew ‚Üí stir_continuously`.
5. Either someone tastes the stew or prepares a salad (inclusive "or"): `taste_stew ‚à® prepare_salad`.

**Given:** The stew is not savory (`¬¨savory`).

**Goal:** Prove `prepare_salad` follows.

**Proof Structure:**
1. From `¬¨savory` and `add_salt ‚Üí savory`, we derive `¬¨add_salt` (modus tollens).
2. From `¬¨add_salt` and `add_salt ‚à® ¬¨thickens_perfectly`, we derive `¬¨thickens_perfectly` (disjunctive syllogism).
3. From `¬¨thickens_perfectly` and `stir_continuously ‚Üí thickens_perfectly`, we derive `¬¨stir_continuously` (modus tollens).
4. From `¬¨stir_continuously` and `taste_stew ‚Üí stir_continuously`, we derive `¬¨taste_stew` (modus tollens).
5. From `¬¨taste_stew` and `taste_stew ‚à® prepare_salad`, we derive `prepare_salad` (disjunctive syllogism).

Thus, the conclusion that someone prepared a salad follows from the premises and the given that the stew is not savory.

theorem salad_prepared : prepare_salad := by
  have h1 : ¬¨ add_salt := fun h_add => given (R1 h_add)
