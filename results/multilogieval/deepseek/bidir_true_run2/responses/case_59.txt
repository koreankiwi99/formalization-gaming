The context describes a person who wanted to play guitar and had specific realizations. The statement to evaluate is that Emma is unable to entertain friends with the guitar, and we must determine if she gains knowledge of music theory.

### Formalization in Lean:
- Introduce a type `Person` and a constant `Emma` for the specific person.
- Predicates:
  - `LearnChords`: learned basic chords (note: strumming patterns are handled separately).
  - `LearnStrumming`: learned strumming patterns.
  - `TakeLessons`: took guitar lessons.
  - `CanPlaySimpleSongs`: can play simple songs.
  - `CanEntertain`: can entertain friends with the guitar.
  - `GainMusicTheory`: gains knowledge of music theory.
- Axioms based on the context:
  1. If someone learns chords **and** strumming, they can play simple songs.
  2. If someone can play simple songs, they can entertain friends.
  3. For any person, they either learned chords, took lessons, or both (disjunction).
  4. If someone takes lessons, they gain music theory.
- Additional fact: Emma cannot entertain friends (`¬CanEntertain Emma`).

axiom Person : Type
axiom Emma : Person
axiom LearnChords : Person → Prop
axiom LearnStrumming : Person → Prop
axiom TakeLessons : Person → Prop
axiom CanPlaySimpleSongs : Person → Prop
axiom CanEntertain : Person → Prop
axiom GainMusicTheory : Person → Prop

-- If a person learns chords and strumming, they can play simple songs
axiom A1 : ∀ p : Person, LearnChords p → LearnStrumming p → CanPlaySimpleSongs p
-- If they can play simple songs, they can entertain
axiom A2 : ∀ p : Person, CanPlaySimpleSongs p → CanEntertain p
-- They learned chords or took lessons (or both)
axiom A3 : ∀ p : Person, LearnChords p ∨ TakeLessons p
-- Taking lessons implies gaining music theory
axiom A4 : ∀ p : Person, TakeLessons p → GainMusicTheory p
-- Emma cannot entertain
axiom Emma_fact : ¬ CanEntertain Emma

-- Attempt to prove Emma gains music theory
theorem try_prove : GainMusicTheory Emma := by
  apply A4 Emma -- sufficient to show TakeLessons Emma
  -- From A2 and Emma_fact, Emma cannot play simple songs
  have no_play : ¬ CanPlaySimpleSongs Emma := fun h => Emma_fact (A2 Emma h)
  -- From A1, not playing implies ¬LearnChords Emma ∨ ¬LearnStrumming Emma
  have : ¬ (LearnChords Emma ∧ LearnStrumming Emma) := 
    fun ⟨h1, h2⟩ => no_play (A1 Emma h1 h2)
  -- A3 gives LearnChords Emma ∨ TakeLessons Emma
  cases (A3 Emma) with
  | inl chords =>
    -- Case 1: Emma learned chords. But we don't know if she learned strumming.
    -- Without strumming, she might not play, but we cannot deduce lessons.
    -- Thus, cannot prove TakeLessons Emma in this case.
    fail "Cannot prove TakeLessons Emma when Emma learned chords but strumming status is unknown"
  | inr lessons => exact lessons -- Case 2: Emma took lessons → proves TakeLessons Emma

-- Since the proof fails in case 1, we cannot fully prove GainMusicTheory Emma
</lean>

  - Emma cannot entertain (`Emma_fact`), so by axiom `A2`, she cannot play simple songs.
  - Axiom `A1` implies that to play simple songs, both chords **and** strumming are required. Thus, Emma lacks at least one (chords, strumming, or both).
  - Axiom `A3` states Emma learned chords **or** took lessons (or both). However, there is **no link** between taking lessons and learning chords/strumming.
- **Proof Attempt**:
  - Case 1: Emma learned chords. Without knowing if she learned strumming, we cannot infer she took lessons (she might lack strumming, explaining her inability to play). Thus, we cannot prove she took lessons.
  - Case 2: Emma took lessons. Then by `A4`, she gains music theory. However, since Case 1 fails, the overall proof fails.
- **Conclusion**: The inability to entertain implies Emma might have never taken lessons (e.g., she learned chords but not strumming). Thus, we **cannot prove** she gains music theory.
