4. If crops grow well, harvest is bountiful.
5. If harvest is bountiful, farmer makes good profit.

**Statement to evaluate:**  
If pests invaded, did the farmer make good profit?  

**Lean formalization:**  
I model the scenario using propositions without additional assumptions. The disjunction (premise 3) guarantees one of the three cases holds. When pests invade:  
- "pests did not damage crops" becomes false (by premise 2)  
- This reduces the disjunction to: *farmer watered adequately* OR *enough water and pests damaged crops*  

However:  
- *farmer watered adequately* does not entail *enough water* (no premise links them)  
- *enough water* would imply good profit (premises 1,4,5)  
- But when pests invade and only *farmer watered adequately* holds (without water sufficiency), profit cannot be guaranteed  

Since one possible case (*farmer watered adequately* without water sufficiency) prevents proving profit, the conclusion fails.
