The reasoning establishes that if Mary's technique improved, her artwork would be high quality (A2). Since her artwork was not high quality, her technique did not improve (contrapositive of A2). If she had practiced often, her technique would have improved (A1), so she did not practice often (contrapositive). Given that she either practiced often or took an art class (A3), and she did not practice often, she must have taken an art class. Taking an art class implies learning new methods (A4), so Mary learned new methods.

axiom Person : Type

axiom TakeArtClass : Person → Prop
