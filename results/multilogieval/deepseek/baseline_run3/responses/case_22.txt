The context states that if a city experiences a drought, authorities should implement water restrictions and ration water usage. The question provides that either water restrictions were not enforced or water rationing did not occur. We need to determine if the city is undergoing a drought.

Formalizing in Lean:
- `drought`: Prop representing whether the city is undergoing a drought.
- `implement_restrictions`: Prop representing whether water restrictions were implemented.
- `ration_water`: Prop representing whether water rationing occurred.
- Axiom `R1`: If drought occurs, restrictions must be implemented.
- Axiom `R2`: If drought occurs, water must be rationed.
- Given: Either restrictions were not implemented or rationing did not occur (`¬implement_restrictions ∨ ¬ration_water`).

The goal is to prove or disprove `drought`.

Analysis:
- If `drought` were true, then by `R1` and `R2`, both `implement_restrictions` and `ration_water` must hold.
- The given disjunction `¬implement_restrictions ∨ ¬ration_water` means at least one of these consequences fails.
- Therefore, `drought` cannot be true, leading to a proof of `¬drought`.

<lean>
axiom drought : Prop
axiom implement_restrictions : Prop
axiom ration_water : Prop
axiom R1 : drought → implement_restrictions
axiom R2 : drought → ration_water
axiom given : ¬ implement_restrictions ∨ ¬ ration_water

theorem not_drought : ¬ drought :=
  fun h : drought =>
  have h1 : implement_restrictions := R1 h
  have h2 : ration_water := R2 h
  given.elim
    (fun not_restrict : ¬ implement_restrictions => not_restrict h1)
    (fun not_ration : ¬ ration_water => not_ration h2)
</lean>

The Lean proof shows `¬drought` is provable from the axioms. Thus, the city is not undergoing a drought.

ANSWER: No
