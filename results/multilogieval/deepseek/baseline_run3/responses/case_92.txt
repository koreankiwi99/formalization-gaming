The context provides several implications and a disjunction. The key elements are:
- Enough water leads to crops growing well.
- Pests invasion leads to crops destruction.
- A disjunction: farmer watered adequately OR no crop destruction OR (enough water AND crop destruction).
- Crops growing well lead to bountiful harvest, which leads to good profit.

The question asks: If pests invaded, did the farmer make good profit?

In Lean, we formalize the propositions and axioms. When attempting to prove "PestsInvade → GoodProfit", we consider:
1. If pests invade, crops are destroyed (A2).
2. The disjunction (A3) simplifies to FarmerWatered ∨ EnoughWater (since ¬CropsDestroyed is false).
3. If EnoughWater holds, we get GoodProfit via A1, A4, A5.
4. If FarmerWatered holds, there is no axiom linking it to EnoughWater or directly to GoodProfit. Thus, we cannot prove GoodProfit in this case.

Since one branch (FarmerWatered) does not lead to GoodProfit, the theorem cannot be proven. Conversely, we cannot prove the negation because there exists a scenario (EnoughWater) where pests invade and GoodProfit holds. Thus, neither the statement nor its negation is provable.
