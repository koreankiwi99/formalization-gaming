- The given disjunction (LindaFact) means at least one of these is true:
  a) ¬PutOnLayers Linda → ¬Cold Linda (directly from contrapositive of R1)
  b) ¬TurnUpHeat Linda → ¬FeelsChill Linda (contrapositive of R2), but this doesn't relate to Cold Linda
- Scenario a) proves ¬Cold Linda, but scenario b) provides no information about Cold Linda

- Assuming Cold Linda leads to PutOnLayers Linda (by R1), which would make the first disjunct false, requiring ¬TurnUpHeat Linda. This is consistent since no axiom forces cold people to turn up heat.

Thus, we have:
- If ¬PutOnLayers Linda holds, then ¬Cold Linda is provable
- If only ¬TurnUpHeat Linda holds, Cold Linda could be true or false

Since the disjunction doesn't specify which case holds, we cannot prove Cold Linda or its negation conclusively.
