The context provides several logical relationships:
- If someone exercises regularly, they are fit: ∀x, ExerciseRegularly x → Fit x
- If someone eats nutritious foods, they have energy: ∀x, EatNutritiousFoods x → HaveEnergy x
- Not everyone is fit or has energy: ∃x, ¬Fit x ∨ ¬HaveEnergy x
- If someone values wellness, they exercise regularly: ∀x, ValuesWellness x → ExerciseRegularly x

Jane eats nutritious foods, so she has energy: EatNutritiousFoods Jane → HaveEnergy Jane. However, there is no direct link between eating nutritious foods and valuing wellness. The axiom about valuing wellness only relates to exercise, not to eating habits. Jane may or may not exercise regularly, and there is no information forcing her to value wellness based on her diet. The existence of someone who isn't fit or energetic doesn't specifically implicate Jane, as she has energy. Thus, we cannot prove Jane values wellness nor its negation.
