The context provides several implications and disjunctions that relate the wetness of the driveway, recent rain, car washing, water usage, hose status, and sponge soapiness. The question asks whether the sponge was soapy given that it didn't rain recently.

**Key reasoning:**
1. From "If the driveway is wet, then it must have rained recently" (Wet → Rained) and the assumption ¬Rained, we derive ¬Wet.
2. From "Either the driveway is wet, or no water was used..." (Wet ∨ ¬Water) and ¬Wet, we derive ¬Water.
3. From "If someone washed their car, then they used water..." (Washed → Water) and ¬Water, we derive ¬Washed.
4. From "If the hose is uncoiled, it means someone was washing..." (Uncoiled → Washed) and ¬Washed, we derive ¬Uncoiled.
5. From "Either the hose is uncoiled, or the sponge is soapy..." (Uncoiled ∨ Soapy) and ¬Uncoiled, we derive Soapy.

Thus, under the assumption of no recent rain, the sponge must be soapy.

<lean>
axiom Rained : Prop
axiom Wet : Prop
axiom Water : Prop
axiom Washed : Prop
axiom Uncoiled : Prop
axiom Soapy : Prop

axiom A1 : Wet → Rained
axiom A2 : Washed → Water
axiom A3 : Wet ∨ ¬Water
axiom A4 : Uncoiled → Washed
axiom A5 : Uncoiled ∨ Soapy

theorem sponge_soapy : ¬Rained → Soapy := by
  intro noRain
  have noWet : ¬Wet := fun wet => noRain (A1 wet)
  cases A3 with
  | inl wet => contradiction
  | inr noWater =>
    have noWashed : ¬Washed := fun washed => noWater (A2 washed)
    have noUncoiled : ¬Uncoiled := fun uncoiled => noWashed (A4 uncoiled)
    cases A5 with
    | inl uncoiled => contradiction
    | inr soapy => exact soapy
</lean>

ANSWER: Yes
