The reasoning involves propositions about the team's practice, weather conditions, children's activities, and the team's confidence. The key is to show that if it is sunny, then the team holds their heads up high, using the given implications and disjunction.

<lean>
axiom Practice : Prop
axiom Prepared : Prop
axiom Sunny : Prop
axiom Play : Prop
axiom Confidence : Prop
axiom HeadHigh : Prop

axiom A1 : Practice → Prepared
axiom A2 : Sunny → Play
axiom A3 : Practice ∨ ¬ Play
axiom A4 : Prepared → Confidence
axiom A5 : Confidence → HeadHigh

theorem sunny_implies_headhigh : Sunny → HeadHigh := by
  intro hSunny
  have hPlay : Play := A2 hSunny
  cases A3 with
  | inl hPractice =>
      have hPrepared : Prepared := A1 hPractice
      have hConfidence : Confidence := A4 hPrepared
      exact A5 hConfidence
  | inr hnPlay =>
      have contra : False := hnPlay hPlay
      contradiction
</lean>

The proof shows that assuming sunny weather leads to children playing at the park. The disjunction (either daily practice or no park play) forces daily practice when children play, triggering the chain: practice → prepared → confident → head held high. The second branch of the disjunction leads to a contradiction when children play. Thus, the theorem holds.
