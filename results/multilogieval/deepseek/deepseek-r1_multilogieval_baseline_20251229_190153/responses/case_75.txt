**Translation and Proof in Lean**

The context and question are translated into Lean by defining the necessary predicates and axioms. The context states a conditional relationship involving preventive maintenance, quality parts, and long service life for a car. The specific information about Liz's car (high-quality parts used, but no long service life) is added as axioms. The goal is to determine whether preventive maintenance was done on Liz's car.

<lean>
axiom Car : Type
axiom lizsCar : Car
axiom PreventiveMaintenance : Car → Prop
axiom QualityParts : Car → Prop
axiom LongServiceLife : Car → Prop
axiom MechanicSaying : ∀ c : Car, PreventiveMaintenance c → (QualityParts c → LongServiceLife c)
axiom HighQualityParts : QualityParts lizsCar
axiom NotLongLife : ¬ LongServiceLife lizsCar

theorem preventive_maintenance_not_done : ¬ PreventiveMaintenance lizsCar :=
  fun h : PreventiveMaintenance lizsCar =>
    have q_imp_l : QualityParts lizsCar → LongServiceLife lizsCar := MechanicSaying lizsCar h
    have contradiction : LongServiceLife lizsCar := q_imp_l HighQualityParts
    NotLongLife contradiction
</lean>

**Explanation of the Proof:**
1. **Assumption of `PreventiveMaintenance lizsCar`:** Temporary assumption (for contradiction).
2. **Apply `MechanicSaying` to Liz's car:** Yields `QualityParts lizsCar → LongServiceLife lizsCar`.
3. **Use `HighQualityParts`:** Together with step 2, this implies `LongServiceLife lizsCar`.
4. **Contradiction:** `LongServiceLife lizsCar` (step 3) contradicts `¬LongServiceLife lizsCar` (`NotLongLife`).
5. **Conclusion:** The assumption `PreventiveMaintenance lizsCar` must be false.

This proves that preventive maintenance was **not** done on Liz's car. Since the original question asks whether maintenance was done, and we proved it was not, the answer to the question is **No**.

ANSWER: No
