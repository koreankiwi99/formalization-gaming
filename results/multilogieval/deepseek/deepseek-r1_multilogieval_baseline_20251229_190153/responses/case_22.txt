The context states that if a city experiences a drought, authorities must implement water restrictions and ration water usage. The given disjunction indicates that either water restrictions were not enforced or water rationing did not occur. To determine if the city is undergoing a drought, we analyze these conditions.

**Formalization:**
- `Drought`: Prop representing whether the city is undergoing a drought.
- `ImplementRestrictions`: Prop representing whether authorities enforce water restrictions.
- `RationWater`: Prop representing whether water usage is rationed.
- Axiom `A1`: Drought implies water restrictions are implemented.
- Axiom `A2`: Drought implies water usage is rationed.
- Given disjunction: ¬ImplementRestrictions ∨ ¬RationWater.

**Proof:**
Assume `Drought` holds. By `A1`, `ImplementRestrictions` must be true. By `A2`, `RationWater` must be true. However, the given disjunction states that at least one of these is false, leading to a contradiction. Therefore, `Drought` must be false.

<lean>
axiom Drought : Prop
axiom ImplementRestrictions : Prop
axiom RationWater : Prop
axiom A1 : Drought → ImplementRestrictions
axiom A2 : Drought → RationWater
axiom Given : ¬ImplementRestrictions ∨ ¬RationWater

theorem not_drought : ¬Drought := by
  intro hDrought
  have hRes : ImplementRestrictions := A1 hDrought
  have hRat : RationWater := A2 hDrought
  cases Given with
  | inl noRes => exact noRes hRes
  | inr noRat => exact noRat hRat
</lean>

ANSWER: No
