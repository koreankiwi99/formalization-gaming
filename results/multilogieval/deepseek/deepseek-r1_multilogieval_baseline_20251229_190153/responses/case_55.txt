The reasoning involves propositions about the movie: good reviews, high attendance, presence of a big star, and being a sequel. The axioms are:

1. Good reviews imply high attendance.
2. Either good reviews or low attendance (or both) must hold.
3. A big star implies good reviews.
4. Either a big star or a sequel (or both) exists.

The question asks whether low attendance implies it's a sequel. From the axioms:
- If attendance is low (¬ManyPeople), axiom 2 still holds but doesn't directly yield new information.
- Low attendance implies no good reviews (contrapositive of axiom 1).
- No good reviews implies no big star (contrapositive of axiom 3).
- With no big star, axiom 4 forces the movie to be a sequel.

Thus, low attendance implies it's a sequel.

<lean>
axiom GoodReviews : Prop
axiom ManyPeople : Prop
axiom BigStar : Prop
axiom Sequel : Prop

axiom A1 : GoodReviews → ManyPeople
axiom A2 : GoodReviews ∨ ¬ManyPeople
axiom A3 : BigStar → GoodReviews
axiom A4 : BigStar ∨ Sequel

theorem low_attendance_implies_sequel : ¬ManyPeople → Sequel :=
  fun h : ¬ManyPeople =>
  have ng : ¬GoodReviews := fun gr : GoodReviews => h (A1 gr)
  have nbs : ¬BigStar := fun bs : BigStar => ng (A3 bs)
  Or.elim A4 (fun bs => False.elim (nbs bs)) (fun s => s)
</lean>

ANSWER: Yes
