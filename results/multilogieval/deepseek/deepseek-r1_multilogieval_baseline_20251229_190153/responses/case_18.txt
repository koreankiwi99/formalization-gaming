The reasoning involves implications and disjunctions. Let's formalize the context:

- Let `Employee` be the type of employees.
- Sam is a specific employee.
- Predicates:
  - `Impressed e`: employee e impressed the manager.
  - `Promotion e`: employee e got a promotion.
  - `Extra e`: employee e took on extra responsibilities.
  - `Experience e`: employee e gained valuable experience.
- `Lead e`: employee e was assigned to lead a new team.

Axioms from context:
1. If someone impresses the manager, they get a promotion: `∀ e, Impressed e → Promotion e`
2. If someone takes extra responsibilities, they gain experience: `∀ e, Extra e → Experience e`
3. Sam either impressed the manager or took extra work (or both): `Impressed Sam ∨ Extra Sam`
4. If someone gains experience, they lead a new team: `∀ e, Experience e → Lead e`

Given: Sam did not get a promotion (`¬Promotion Sam`).

We need to determine if Sam was assigned to lead a new team (`Lead Sam`).

<lean>
axiom Employee : Type
axiom Sam : Employee
axiom Impressed : Employee → Prop
axiom Promotion : Employee → Prop
axiom Extra : Employee → Prop
axiom Experience : Employee → Prop
axiom Lead : Employee → Prop

axiom A1 : ∀ e, Impressed e → Promotion e
axiom A2 : ∀ e, Extra e → Experience e
axiom A3 : Impressed Sam ∨ Extra Sam
axiom A4 : ∀ e, Experience e → Lead e
axiom Given : ¬ Promotion Sam

theorem sam_leads : Lead Sam := by
  -- Since Sam didn't get promotion, they didn't impress manager (by contrapositive of A1)
  have h_no_impress : ¬ Impressed Sam := fun h_impress => Given (A1 Sam h_impress)
  -- From A3 (Sam either impressed or took extra work), and since they didn't impress, they must have taken extra work
  have h_extra : Extra Sam := by
    cases A3 with
    | inl h_imp => contradiction
    | inr h_extra => exact h_extra
  -- By A2, taking extra work gives experience
  have h_exp : Experience Sam := A2 Sam h_extra
  -- By A4, experience leads to team leadership
  exact A4 Sam h_exp
</lean>

The proof shows that:
1. Sam didn't impress the manager (because no promotion, via axiom A1).
2. Since Sam either impressed or took extra work (axiom A3), they must have taken extra work.
3. Taking extra work implies gaining experience (axiom A2).
4. Gaining experience implies leading a new team (axiom A4).

Therefore, Sam was assigned to lead a new team.

ANSWER: Yes
