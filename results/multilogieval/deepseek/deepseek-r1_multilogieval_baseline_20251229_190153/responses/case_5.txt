The context provides the following information:
1. Home owners (like Mary) are the subject.
2. Learning about botany → understanding plant needs → growing thriving plants.
3. Each home homeowner did at least one (possibly both) of: learning botany or seeking advice.

Given that Mary's plants did not thrive, we can deduce:
- She did not grow thriving plants.
- If she had learned botany, she would have grown thriving plants (by the implications).
- Therefore, she did not learn botany.
- Since she must have done at least one (learn botany or seek advice), she must have sought advice.

This is formalized in Lean:

<lean>
axiom Person : Type
axiom Mary : Person
axiom learned_botany : Person → Prop
axiom sought_advice : Person → Prop
axiom understand_needs : Person → Prop
axiom grow_thriving_plants : Person → Prop
axiom botany_implies_understanding : ∀ p, learned_botany p → understand_needs p
axiom understanding_implies_thriving : ∀ p, understand_needs p → grow_thriving_plants p
axiom at_least_one : ∀ p, learned_botany p ∨ sought_advice p
axiom mary_not_thriving : ¬ grow_thriving_plants Mary

theorem mary_sought_advice : sought_advice Mary :=
  by
    have no_botany : ¬ learned_botany Mary := 
      fun h_botany => 
        mary_not_thriving (understanding_implies_thriving Mary (botany_implies_understanding Mary h_botany))
    have disj : learned_botany Mary ∨ sought_advice Mary := at_least_one Mary
    cases disj
    . contradiction
    . assumption
</lean>
