<lean>
axiom Person : Type
axiom Mark : Person
axiom WakesUpEarly : Person → Prop
axiom Energized : Person → Prop
axiom PlansDay : Person → Prop
axiom Productive : Person → Prop
axiom SelfDiscipline : Person → Prop

-- If someone wakes up early, they feel energized
axiom A1 : ∀ p : Person, WakesUpEarly p → Energized p
-- If someone plans their day, they are productive
axiom A2 : ∀ p : Person, PlansDay p → Productive p
-- Some people lack energy or productivity
axiom A3 : ∃ p : Person, ¬Energized p ∨ ¬Productive p
-- If someone has self-discipline, they wake up early
axiom A4 : ∀ p : Person, SelfDiscipline p → WakesUpEarly p

-- Given that Mark plans his day well
axiom MarkPlans : PlansDay Mark

-- We wish to prove/disprove: SelfDiscipline Mark

-- From MarkPlans and A2, Mark is productive
theorem MarkProductive : Productive Mark := A2 Mark MarkPlans

-- We cannot prove SelfDiscipline Mark because:
-- 1. There's no axiom linking planning/day or productivity to self-discipline
-- 2. A3 shows some people lack energy/productivity, but Mark is productive so this doesn't constrain him
-- 3. A4 only gives implications FROM self-discipline, not toward it

-- We also cannot prove ¬SelfDiscipline Mark:
-- Mark being productive and planning his day doesn't contradict having self-discipline
-- In fact, Mark could have self-discipline (leading to waking early and energized) while also planning his day

-- Therefore, neither SelfDiscipline Mark nor its negation is provable
</lean>
