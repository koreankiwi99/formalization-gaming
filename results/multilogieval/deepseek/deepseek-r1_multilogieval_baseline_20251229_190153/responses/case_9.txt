The reasoning involves propositions about the party. We define:
- `P`: It is a birthday party.
- `B`: Someone brought a dish to share.
- `E`: There is enough food for everyone.
- `C`: There is cake.

Axioms:
1. Bringing a dish implies enough food: `B → E`.
2. Birthday party implies cake and ice cream: `P → (C ∧ I)` (ice cream `I` is not needed for the conclusion).
3. Either a dish was brought or no cake: `B ∨ ¬C`.

Given `P` (Sam's birthday party), we prove `E`:
- From `P` and axiom 2, `C` holds.
- With `C` true, axiom 3 forces `B` to be true (since `¬C` is false).
- Then `B` and axiom 1 imply `E`.

Lean proof:

<lean>
axiom P : Prop
axiom B : Prop
axiom E : Prop
axiom C : Prop
axiom I : Prop

axiom A1 : B → E
axiom A2 : P → (C ∧ I)
axiom A3 : B ∨ ¬C

theorem sam_enough_food : P → E := by
  intro hP
  have hC : C := (A2 hP).left
  cases A3 with
  | inl hB => exact A1 hB
  | inr notC => contradiction
</lean>
