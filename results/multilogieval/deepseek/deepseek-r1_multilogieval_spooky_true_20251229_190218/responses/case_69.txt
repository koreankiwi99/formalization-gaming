The context states a universal principle among athletes: if a player trains hard and remains focused, they perform to the best of their ability. Given that Lebron remained focused but did not perform best, we prove he did not train hard.

<lean>
axiom Player : Type
axiom Lebron : Player
axiom TrainsHard : Player → Prop
axiom RemainsFocused : Player → Prop
axiom PerformsBest : Player → Prop

axiom coaching : ∀ (p : Player), TrainsHard p → RemainsFocused p → PerformsBest p
axiom focused : RemainsFocused Lebron
axiom not_best : ¬ PerformsBest Lebron

theorem not_trained : ¬ TrainsHard Lebron :=
  fun (h_train : TrainsHard Lebron) =>
    have h_best : PerformsBest Lebron := coaching Lebron h_train focused
    not_best h_best
</lean>

The proof shows that assuming Lebron trained hard leads to a contradiction with the given facts. Therefore, Lebron did not train hard.
