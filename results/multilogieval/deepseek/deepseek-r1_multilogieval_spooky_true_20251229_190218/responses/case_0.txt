The reasoning involves translating the natural language statements into logical propositions and proving whether "they bought their dream home" follows from the premises, given that they could not afford a down payment.

**Key premises:**
1. Saved aggressively → Afford down payment
2. Improved credit → Qualify for mortgage
3. Saved aggressively ∨ Improved credit
4. Qualify for mortgage → Buy dream home
5. **Given:** Could not afford down payment (¬Afford down payment)

**Proof:**
- From (1) and (5), by contraposition: ¬Saved aggressively
- From (3) and ¬Saved aggressively: Improved credit
- From (2) and Improved credit: Qualify for mortgage
- From (4) and Qualify for mortgage: Buy dream home

Thus, the conclusion "they bought their dream home" is provable.

<lean>
axiom obj : Type
axiom Person : obj

axiom Saved : obj → Prop
axiom Afford : obj → Prop
axiom Improved : obj → Prop
axiom Qualified : obj → Prop
axiom Buy : obj → Prop

axiom P1 : ∀ x, Saved x → Afford x
axiom P2 : ∀ x, Improved x → Qualified x
axiom P3 : Saved Person ∨ Improved Person
axiom P4 : ∀ x, Qualified x → Buy x
axiom H_given : ¬ Afford Person

theorem bought_dream_home : Buy Person := by
  apply P4 Person  -- To show Qualified Person (from mortgage qualification implies buying)
  apply P2 Person  -- To show Improved Person (since improved credit implies qualification)
  have not_saved : ¬ Saved Person := fun h_saved => H_given (P1 Person h_saved)
  cases P3 with
  | inl saved => contradiction
  | inr improved => exact improved
</lean>

ANSWER: Yes
