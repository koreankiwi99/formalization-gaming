ANSWER: Failure

**Explanation of Failure**:  
The premises don't provide sufficient connections to prove the conclusion:
1. When peaches are in season (`season` case), there's no axiom linking this to ripe peaches being available at the market, so we cannot trigger pie baking.
2. When someone wins a contest (`winner` case):
   - If the winner is James, we get a contradiction (since he must celebrate), which proves anything (including homeyCozy).
   - If the winner isn't James, their celebration doesn't imply pie baking (P2 only gives celebration, not baking), so we cannot derive homeyCozy.

Since two cases (`season` and non-James `winner`) remain unprovable, the overall implication cannot be established from the given premises.
