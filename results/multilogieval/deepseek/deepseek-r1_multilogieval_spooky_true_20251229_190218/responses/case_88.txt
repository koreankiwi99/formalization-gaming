The reasoning involves proving that if the cake batter contained eggs, then the guests will be happy. The premises establish:
1. Correct oven temperature (T) ensures proper baking (B).
2. Batter with eggs (E) ensures fluffiness (F), though fluffiness isn't needed for the conclusion.
3. Either T is true or E is false.
4. Proper baking (B) ensures delicious taste (D).
5. Delicious taste (D) ensures happy guests (H).

Assuming E (batter contains eggs), premise 3 forces T to be true (since ¬E is false). Then:
- T → B (premise 1) → B
- B → D (premise 4) → D
- D → H (premise 5) → H

Thus, E → H is provable.

<lean>
axiom E : Prop
axiom T : Prop
axiom B : Prop
axiom D : Prop
axiom H : Prop
axiom F : Prop

axiom P1 : T → B
axiom P2 : E → F
axiom P3 : T ∨ ¬E
axiom P4 : B → D
axiom P5 : D → H

theorem eggs_imply_happy : E → H := by
  intro hE
  rcases P3 with hT | hnE
  . exact P5 (P4 (P1 hT))
  . contradiction
</lean>
