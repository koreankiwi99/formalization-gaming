The context provides information about athletes and their dedication, training, nutrition, and performance issues. However, the statement about Mark eating a healthy diet does not directly imply dedication to his sport based on the given premises. 

Premises:
1. Proper training enhances skills.
2. Healthy nutrition increases strength.
3. Athletes lack skill or are deficient in strength.
4. Dedication leads to proper training.

Mark's healthy diet implies increased strength (from premise 2), so he is not deficient in strength. By premise 3, he must lack skill. If Mark were dedicated, he would train properly (premise 4) and enhance his skills (premise 1), contradicting the lack of skill. Thus, Mark cannot be dedicated. Since we cannot prove dedication and instead prove the opposite, the conclusion "Mark is dedicated" cannot be established from the context.

axiom lacking_skill : Athlete → Prop

axiom T3 : ∀ a : Athlete, lacking_skill a ∨ deficient_strength a
axiom T4 : ∀ a : Athlete, dedicated a → train_properly a

-- Implicit: Enhanced skills negate lacking skill, increased strength negates deficient strength
axiom T5 : ∀ a : Athlete, enhance_skills a → ¬ lacking_skill a
axiom T6 : ∀ a : Athlete, increase_strength a → ¬ deficient_strength a
