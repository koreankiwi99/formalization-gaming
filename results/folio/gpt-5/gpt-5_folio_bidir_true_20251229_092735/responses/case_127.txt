axiom InvitesFriends : Person → Prop

-- All people who make wedding plans are engaged.

-- All people who invite others to their ceremony make wedding plans.
axiom A2 : ∀ x : Person, InvitesOthers x → MakePlans x
-- Anyone who has a well-attended wedding invites others to their ceremony.
axiom A3 : ∀ x : Person, WellAttended x → InvitesOthers x
-- Some people that have well-attended weddings have larger families.
axiom A4 : ∃ x : Person, WellAttended x ∧ LargerFamily x
-- If John is engaged, then John does not (invite friends to their ceremony and make wedding plans).
axiom A5 : Engaged John → ¬ (InvitesFriends John ∧ MakePlans John)
-- If John has a larger family, then either John has a well-attended wedding or invites friends to their ceremony.
axiom A6 : LargerFamily John → (WellAttended John ∨ InvitesFriends John)

-- From these premises, there is no derivation that concludes LargerFamily John.
-- The existential A4 does not specify John, and no premises connect John to those individuals.
-- Hence we cannot prove: LargerFamily John.
