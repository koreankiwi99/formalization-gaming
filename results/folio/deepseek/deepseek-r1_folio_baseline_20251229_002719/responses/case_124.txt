The scenario involves Peter's pets, mammals, animals, monkeys, birds, flying, breathing, and wings. Rock is Peter's pet. The axioms provide:

- Peter's pets that are mammals are animals.
- Monkeys are mammals.
- Peter's pets are monkeys or birds (inclusive OR).
- Peter's birds can fly.
- Animals that are Peter's pets can breathe.
- If a Peter's pet can fly, it has wings.
- Rock can fly, is a bird, or cannot breathe.

The disjunction "Rock can fly, or Rock is a bird, or Rock cannot breathe" does not force Rock to be a monkey. If Rock is a monkey, it must breathe (since monkeys are mammals, hence animals, hence breathing), so "cannot breathe" is false, leaving "can fly" or "is a bird". But Rock might not fly, and being a bird doesn't contradict being a monkey (without mutual-exclusivity axioms). Also, Rock could be only a bird: then it flies by axiom 4, satisfying the disjunction. Rock being only a bird doesn't require breathing (axiom 5 applies only to animals, and birds aren't guaranteed to be animals). Thus, both possibilities (Rock is a monkey or Rock is only a bird) are consistent with the axioms. Therefore, it's uncertain whether Rock is a monkey.
