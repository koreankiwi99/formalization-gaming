The reasoning involves evaluating the statement: "If Tom is not both a grumpy person and mean to animals, then Tom is neither a grumpy person nor an animal lover." Using the given context:

1. Tom is a pet owner (axiom T5).
2. All pet owners are animal lovers (axiom R4), so Tom is an animal lover.
3. All animal lovers are nice to animals (axiom R3), so Tom is nice to animals.
4. No one nice to animals is mean to animals (axiom R1), so Tom is not mean to animals.
5. The antecedent "Tom is not both a grumpy person and mean to animals" is true because Tom is not mean to animals.
6. The consequent "Tom is neither a grumpy person nor an animal lover" is false because Tom is an animal lover.

Since the antecedent is true and the consequent is false, the entire implication is false.

axiom AnimalLover : obj → Prop

theorem Tom_animal_lover : AnimalLover Tom := R4 Tom T5

theorem Tom_not_mean : ¬ MeanToAnimals Tom := R1 Tom Tom_nice

theorem antecedent_true : ¬(Grumpy Tom ∧ MeanToAnimals Tom) :=
  fun h => Tom_not_mean h.2

theorem consequent_false : ¬(¬Grumpy Tom ∧ ¬AnimalLover Tom) :=
  fun h => h.2 Tom_animal_lover

theorem implication_false : ¬(¬(Grumpy Tom ∧ MeanToAnimals Tom) → (¬Grumpy Tom ∧ ¬AnimalLover Tom)) :=
