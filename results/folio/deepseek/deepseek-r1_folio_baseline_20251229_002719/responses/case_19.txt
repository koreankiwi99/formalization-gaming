The context provides information about animals spotted near the campus and their properties. Rockie is spotted and calm, and we need to evaluate the conditional statement: "If Rockie is not both a turtle and a squirrel, then Rockie is either cute or skittish."

Key deductions:
1. **Rockie is spotted** (from context).
2. **Only rabbits or squirrels are spotted** (from context), so Rockie must be a rabbit or squirrel.
3. **Rockie is calm** (from context), and since skittish implies not calm (from context), Rockie cannot be skittish.
4. If Rockie is a squirrel and spotted, then Rockie must be skittish (from context). But Rockie is calm (not skittish), so Rockie **cannot be a squirrel**.
5. Thus, Rockie must be a rabbit (from step 2 and 4).
6. All spotted rabbits are cute (from context), so Rockie **is cute**.

Since Rockie is cute, the consequent "Rockie is either cute or skittish" is true regardless of the antecedent. Thus, the entire implication holds.

axiom Spotted : obj → Prop
axiom Rabbit : obj → Prop
axiom Squirrel : obj → Prop
axiom Turtle : obj → Prop
axiom Cute : obj → Prop

theorem cute_or_skittish_condition : (¬(Turtle Rockie ∧ Squirrel Rockie)) → (Cute Rockie ∨ Skittish Rockie) := by
  -- Rockie is spotted and calm by axiom
  have spottedRockie : Spotted Rockie := rockie_spotted_calm.left
