2. **Contradiction for Antecedent**:
   - If Breaking Bad gave out roses, it would be a reality TV show (from the chain above).
   - But Breaking Bad is not a reality TV show (Premise 6), so it cannot give out roses.

3. **Conditional Truth**:
   - The conclusion "If roses are given out during Breaking Bad, then it is on Monday at 8 pm" has a false antecedent (Breaking Bad cannot give out roses).
   - A conditional with a false antecedent is always true, regardless of the consequent.

Premise 1 (existence of a Monday 8 pm rose-giving show) is unused but consistent. The conclusion is provable.

axiom obj : Type

theorem breaking_bad_implication : GivesRoses BreakingBad → Monday8pm BreakingBad :=
