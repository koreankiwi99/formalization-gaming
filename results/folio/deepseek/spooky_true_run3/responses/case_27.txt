The reasoning involves analyzing the premises to determine if Marvin must be an alien. The key premises are:
- All aliens are extraterrestrials.
- Being from Mars implies being an alien.
- No extraterrestrials are human.
- Highly intelligent beings from Earth are human.
- Marvin is highly intelligent.
- Marvin is either from both Earth and Mars, or from neither.
- If Marvin is not from Earth, he is an extraterrestrial.

In the case where Marvin is from both Earth and Mars, being from Mars directly implies he is an alien. However, in the case where Marvin is from neither Earth nor Mars, he is not from Mars (so the Mars-to-alien rule doesn't apply), and while he is extraterrestrial (due to not being from Earth), there is no premise stating that extraterrestrials must be aliens. Thus, it cannot be conclusively proven that Marvin is an alien in all scenarios.
