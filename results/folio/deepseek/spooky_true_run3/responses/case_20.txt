Premises:
1. All spotted rabbits are cute.
2. Some spotted turtles exist.
3. Only rabbits and squirrels are spotted.
4. Skittish implies not calm.
5. All spotted squirrels are skittish.
6. Rockie is spotted and calm.

Analysis:
- From premise 6, Rockie is spotted and calm.
- Premise 3 implies Rockie is either a rabbit or a squirrel.
- If Rockie is a squirrel, premise 5 makes Rockie skittish, but premise 4 and 6 lead to a contradiction (calm and not calm). Thus, Rockie cannot be a squirrel.
- Therefore, Rockie must be a rabbit (from premise 3).
- Premise 1 then implies Rockie is cute (since spotted and rabbit).
- So Rockie is cute and calm (antecedent true).
- The consequent requires Rockie to be a skittish turtle. But Rockie is a rabbit (not a turtle) and calm (so not skittish by premise 4). Thus, the consequent is false.
- The implication (true → false) is false.
