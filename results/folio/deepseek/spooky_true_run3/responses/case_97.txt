The reasoning involves evaluating whether the statement "If the Harvard Weekly Book club is a private company, then it either has legal obligations or is created under law" follows from the given premises. The proof proceeds as follows:

1. **Premise Analysis**:
   - All private companies are companies (Premise 3).
   - All companies are business organizations (Premise 2).
   - All business organizations are legal entities (Premise 1).
   - All legal entities have legal obligations (Premise 5).

2. **Proof Strategy**:
   - Assume the Harvard Weekly Book Club (HWBC) is a private company.
   - By Premise 3, HWBC is a company.
   - By Premise 2, HWBC is a business organization.
   - By Premise 1, HWBC is a legal entity.
   - By Premise 5, HWBC has legal obligations.
   - Therefore, the disjunction "has legal obligations or is created under law" holds (specifically, the first disjunct is true).

3. **Unused Premises**:
   - Premise 4 (all legal entities are created under law) and Premise 6 (if created under law then not private) are not required for this proof, as the conclusion follows from the chain of implications alone.

<lean>
axiom obj : Type
axiom HWBC : obj
axiom BusinessOrganization : obj → Prop
axiom Company : obj → Prop
axiom PrivateCompany : obj → Prop
axiom LegalEntity : obj → Prop
axiom CreatedUnderLaw : obj → Prop
axiom HasLegalObligations : obj → Prop

axiom P1 : ∀ x : obj, BusinessOrganization x → LegalEntity x
axiom P2 : ∀ x : obj, Company x → BusinessOrganization x
axiom P3 : ∀ x : obj, PrivateCompany x → Company x
axiom P5 : ∀ x : obj, LegalEntity x → HasLegalObligations x

theorem target : PrivateCompany HWBC → (HasLegalObligations HWBC ∨ CreatedUnderLaw HWBC) :=
  fun h : PrivateCompany HWBC =>
  have comp : Company HWBC := P3 HWBC h
  have bo : BusinessOrganization HWBC := P2 HWBC comp
  have le : LegalEntity HWBC := P1 HWBC bo
  have oblig : HasLegalObligations HWBC := P5 HWBC le
  show HasLegalObligations HWBC ∨ CreatedUnderLaw HWBC from Or.inl oblig
</lean>

ANSWER: True
