The Lean formalization below translates the given premises into logical axioms and attempts to prove that "John does not have a larger family". The proof strategy assumes John has a larger family and derives a contradiction from the premises. However, after careful analysis, no contradiction is found under either interpretation of premise 5 (whether it negates the conjunction or separates the negation). Premise 4 provides an existential statement about some individuals but does not constrain John specifically. The cases derived from premise 6 (well-attended wedding or inviting friends) do not lead to an inconsistency with the other premises. Thus, the proof fails.

<lean>
axiom Person : Type
axiom John : Person
axiom MakesWeddingPlans : Person → Prop
axiom Engaged : Person → Prop
axiom InvitesOthers : Person → Prop
axiom HasWellAttendedWedding : Person → Prop
axiom HasLargerFamily : Person → Prop
axiom InvitesFriends : Person → Prop

-- Premise 1: All people who make wedding plans are engaged.
axiom P1 : ∀ x : Person, MakesWeddingPlans x → Engaged x
-- Premise 2: All who invite others to their ceremony make wedding plans.
axiom P2 : ∀ x : Person, InvitesOthers x → MakesWeddingPlans x
-- Premise 3: Anyone with a well-attended wedding invites others.
axiom P3 : ∀ x : Person, HasWellAttendedWedding x → InvitesOthers x
-- Premise 4: Some with well-attended weddings have larger families (not used for John).
axiom P4 : ∃ x : Person, HasWellAttendedWedding x ∧ HasLargerFamily x
-- Premise 5a: If engaged, John does not (invite friends AND make wedding plans).
axiom P5a : Engaged John → ¬ (InvitesFriends John ∧ MakesWeddingPlans John)
-- Premise 5b: Alternative interpretation (if engaged, John does not invite friends AND makes wedding plans).
axiom P5b : Engaged John → (¬ InvitesFriends John) ∧ MakesWeddingPlans John
-- Premise 6: If John has a larger family, he has a well-attended wedding or invites friends.
axiom P6 : HasLargerFamily John → (HasWellAttendedWedding John ∨ InvitesFriends John)

-- Attempt to prove ¬HasLargerFamily John using P5a (fails).
theorem proof_attempt_P5a : ¬ HasLargerFamily John := by
  intro hLarger
  have h6 := P6 hLarger
  cases h6 with
  | inl hWell =>
    have hInvOthers := P3 John hWell
    have hPlans := P2 John hInvOthers
    have hEngaged := P1 John hPlans
    have h5 := P5a hEngaged
    -- No contradiction: h5 only blocks inviting friends if also making plans, but we lack InvitesFriends.
    admit
  | inr hInvFriends =>
    -- No rule forces engaged or plans from inviting friends alone. Avoid contradiction by not having plans.
    admit

-- Attempt to prove ¬HasLargerFamily John using P5b (fails).
theorem proof_attempt_P5b : ¬ HasLargerFamily John := by
  intro hLarger
  have h6 := P6 hLarger
  cases h6 with
  | inl hWell =>
    have hInvOthers := P3 John hWell
    have hPlans := P2 John hInvOthers
    have hEngaged := P1 John hPlans
    have h5 := P5b hEngaged
    -- ¬InvitesFriends John is consistent; no contradiction.
    admit
  | inr hInvFriends =>
    by_cases hEngaged : Engaged John
    · have h5 := P5b hEngaged
      exact h5.1 hInvFriends  -- Contradiction: ¬InvitesFriends vs hInvFriends.
    · -- Not engaged: no constraint from P5b. Inviting friends without plans is allowed.
      admit

-- Both proof attempts fail; conclusion cannot be proven.
</lean>

ANSWER: Failure
