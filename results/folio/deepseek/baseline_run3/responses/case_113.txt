The reasoning involves analyzing the location of the Picuris Mountains and the implications of the donated mine. The key points are:
1. The Picuris Mountains are in New Mexico or Texas.
2. The Harding Pegmatite Mine is in the Picuris Mountains and was donated.
3. No mountain range in Texas has a donated mine.

If the Picuris Mountains were in Texas, this would contradict the third point because the mine is donated. Therefore, the Picuris Mountains must be in New Mexico. Thus, the statement "The Harding Pegmatite Mine is not located in a mountain range in New Mexico" is false.

<lean>
axiom MountainRange : Type
axiom Mine : Type
axiom Picuris : MountainRange
axiom Harding : Mine
axiom InNM : MountainRange → Prop
axiom InTexas : MountainRange → Prop
axiom LocatedIn : Mine → MountainRange → Prop
axiom Donated : Mine → Prop

axiom A1 : InNM Picuris ∨ InTexas Picuris
axiom A2 : LocatedIn Harding Picuris
axiom A3 : Donated Harding
axiom A4 : ∀ (M : MountainRange), InTexas M → ¬ ∃ (m : Mine), LocatedIn m M ∧ Donated m

theorem picuris_in_nm : InNM Picuris := by
  cases A1 with
  | inl h => exact h
  | inr h =>
      have contra : ¬ ∃ m, LocatedIn m Picuris ∧ Donated m := A4 Picuris h
      apply False.elim
      apply contra
      exists Harding
      exact ⟨A2, A3⟩

-- The statement "The Harding Pegmatite Mine is not located in a mountain range in New Mexico"
-- is equivalent to ¬InNM Picuris. Since we proved InNM Picuris, this statement is false.
</lean>

ANSWER: False
