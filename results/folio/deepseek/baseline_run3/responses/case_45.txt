The context states that all cats are mammals and that there are some pets that are not mammals. The question asks whether we can conclude that no pets are cats.

- **All cats are mammals**: This means if something is a cat, it must be a mammal. Formally: ∀x, Cat x → Mammal x.
- **Some pets are not mammals**: This means there exists at least one pet that is not a mammal. Formally: ∃x, Pet x ∧ ¬Mammal x.
- **No pets are cats**: This would mean that if something is a pet, it cannot be a cat. Formally: ∀x, Pet x → ¬Cat x.

**Analysis**:
- From "some pets are not mammals", we know there is a pet (call it a) that is not a mammal.
- Since a is not a mammal, and all cats are mammals, a cannot be a cat (because if it were, it would be a mammal).
- However, this only tells us that this particular pet (a) is not a cat. It does not rule out the possibility that other pets might be cats.
- There could exist another pet that is a cat (and therefore a mammal), which would contradict "no pets are cats".
- Conversely, there might be no pets that are cats, but the given information does not force this either.
- Therefore, we cannot prove that "no pets are cats" is true, nor can we prove it is false.
