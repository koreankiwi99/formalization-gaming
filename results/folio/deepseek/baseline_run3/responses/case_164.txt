The reasoning involves determining if Breaking Bad gives out roses on Monday at 8 pm based on the given context.

- There exists a show airing Monday at 8 pm that gives out roses (existential claim).
- Any show giving out roses is an episode of The Bachelor.
- The Bachelor portrays real lives (implying every episode does).
- Shows portraying real lives are reality TV.
- Breaking Bad is not a reality TV show.

If Breaking Bad gave out roses, it would be an episode of The Bachelor (by implication), hence portray real lives, and thus be a reality TV show. But Breaking Bad is explicitly not a reality TV show, so it cannot give out roses. Therefore, the statement that Breaking Bad gives out roses on Monday at 8 pm is false, regardless of its airing time.

<lean>
axiom Show : Type

axiom AirsMonday8 : Show → Prop
axiom GivesRoses : Show → Prop
axiom IsBachelorEpisode : Show → Prop
axiom PortraysRealPeople : Show → Prop
axiom IsRealityTV : Show → Prop

axiom BreakingBad : Show

axiom A1 : ∃ s : Show, AirsMonday8 s ∧ GivesRoses s
axiom A2 : ∀ s : Show, GivesRoses s → IsBachelorEpisode s
axiom A3 : ∀ s : Show, IsBachelorEpisode s → PortraysRealPeople s
axiom A4 : ∀ s : Show, PortraysRealPeople s → IsRealityTV s
axiom A5 : ¬ IsRealityTV BreakingBad

theorem breaking_bad_no_roses : ¬ GivesRoses BreakingBad :=
  fun h : GivesRoses BreakingBad =>
  have isBachelor : IsBachelorEpisode BreakingBad := A2 BreakingBad h
  have portrays : PortraysRealPeople BreakingBad := A3 BreakingBad isBachelor
  have reality : IsRealityTV BreakingBad := A4 BreakingBad portrays
  A5 reality

theorem not_roses_monday : ¬ (GivesRoses BreakingBad ∧ AirsMonday8 BreakingBad) :=
  fun h => breaking_bad_no_roses h.left
</lean>

ANSWER: False
