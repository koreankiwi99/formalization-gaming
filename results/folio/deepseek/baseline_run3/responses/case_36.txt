The context states that composers write music pieces and Beethoven wrote Symphony No. 9, which is a music piece. However, the axiom about composers (R1) only states that if someone is a composer, they write at least one music piece. It does not state that writing a music piece implies being a composer. Therefore, from Beethoven writing a music piece, we cannot deduce that he is a composer. Additionally, while Beethoven leads an orchestra (Vienna Music Society) and thus is a conductor (by R2), there is no information connecting conductors to composers. Hence, we cannot prove that Beethoven is a composer. There is also no information contradicting Beethoven being a composer, so we cannot prove the negation. Thus, the statement is uncertain.
