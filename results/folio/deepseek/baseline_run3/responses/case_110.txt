The context states that all fir trees are evergreens and some objects of worship are fir trees. The question asks whether some evergreens are not objects of worship.

From the premises:
1. There exists at least one object of worship that is a fir tree (and therefore an evergreen).
2. However, this does not preclude the possibility that all evergreens are objects of worship. For example, there might be other evergreens (non-fir trees) that are also objects of worship, or the only evergreens might be fir trees that are all objects of worship.
3. Conversely, there might exist evergreens that are not objects of worship (e.g., a fir tree not worshipped or a non-fir evergreen tree not worshipped), but the premises do not require this.

Thus, the statement "some evergreens are not objects of worship" is neither provable nor disprovable from the given information.
