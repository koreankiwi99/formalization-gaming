Based on the context:
- Breeding back is defined as artificial selection.
- Heck cattle were bred back (i.e., underwent breeding back).
- Therefore, Heck cattle underwent artificial selection.

The statement "Some Heck cattle are artificially selected" follows directly because:
1. All Heck cattle were bred back (as stated).
2. Breeding back is artificial selection.
3. Thus, all Heck cattle are artificially selected, implying "some" exist (assuming at least one Heck cattle exists, which is implied by their historical breeding).

<lean>
axiom obj : Type
axiom HeckCattle : obj → Prop
axiom ArtificiallySelected : obj → Prop
axiom BredBack : obj → Prop

-- Axioms from context
axiom breeding_back_is_artificial_selection : ∀ x, BredBack x → ArtificiallySelected x
axiom heck_cattle_bred_back : ∀ x, HeckCattle x → BredBack x
axiom exists_heck_cattle : ∃ x, HeckCattle x  -- Implied by historical breeding

theorem some_heck_artificially_selected : ∃ x, HeckCattle x ∧ ArtificiallySelected x := by
  rcases exists_heck_cattle with ⟨a, h_heck⟩
  have h_bred : BredBack a := heck_cattle_bred_back a h_heck
  have h_art : ArtificiallySelected a := breeding_back_is_artificial_selection a h_bred
  exact ⟨a, h_heck, h_art⟩
