The reasoning involves determining whether James does not have lunch in the company building. The context provides several logical relationships:

1. Employees who schedule meetings go to the building.
2. Employees who lunch in the building schedule meetings.
3. Employees lunch either in the building or at home.
4. Lunch at home implies working remotely.
5. Employees in other countries work remotely.
6. Managers do not work remotely.
7. James appears in the building iff he is a manager.

James is an employee. The key deduction is:
- If James is a manager, he appears in the building (by 7). If he lunched at home, he would work remotely (by 4), contradicting 6. Thus, he must lunch in the building.
- If James is not a manager, he does not appear in the building (by 7). If he lunched in the building, he would schedule meetings (by 2) and thus go to the building (by 1), contradicting non-appearance. Thus, he must lunch at home.

Therefore, James lunches in the building iff he is a manager. Since it is unknown whether James is a manager, the statement "James does not have lunch in the company" is uncertain.
