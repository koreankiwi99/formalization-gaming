Based on the given context:
- All students who want a long vacation have summer as their favorite season.
- James wants a long vacation.
- Emma's favorite season is summer.
- Mia's favorite season is not summer (since it differs from Emma's).

Using the universal statement, since James wants a long vacation, his favorite season must be summer. The information about Emma and Mia supports the consistency of the axioms but is not needed to prove the conclusion about James.

<lean>
inductive Student where
  | emma
  | mia
  | james

inductive Season where
  | spring
  | summer
  | fall
  | winter

axiom favoriteSeason : Student → Season
axiom WantsLongVacation : Student → Prop

-- All students who want long vacation have summer as favorite
axiom A1 : ∀ s, WantsLongVacation s → favoriteSeason s = Season.summer
-- Emma's favorite is summer
axiom A2 : favoriteSeason Student.emma = Season.summer
-- Mia's favorite differs from Emma's (so not summer)
axiom A3 : favoriteSeason Student.mia ≠ favoriteSeason Student.emma
-- James wants long vacation
axiom A4 : WantsLongVacation Student.james

-- James' favorite season must be summer
theorem JamesSummer : favoriteSeason Student.james = Season.summer := by
  apply A1  -- Instantiates the universal for James
  exact A4  -- Uses James' vacation desire
</lean>

ANSWER: True
