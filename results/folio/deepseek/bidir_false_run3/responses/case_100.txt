The statement to evaluate is a conditional: "If Harry is either both a vertebrate and an animal with a backbone, or neither a vertebrate nor an animal with a backbone, then Harry is neither an invertebrate nor a queen bee." Using the given context, we formalize the predicates and derive the following:

- Harry is a bee, so he does not reproduce by male-and-female mating (by axiom A4).
- If Harry had a backbone, he would reproduce by male-and-female mating (by axiom A3), leading to a contradiction. Thus, Harry has no backbone (theorem `backbone_Harry`).
- Vertebrates have backbones (by axiom A2), so lacking a backbone means Harry is not a vertebrate (theorem `vert_Harry`).
- Animals are either invertebrates or vertebrates (axiom A1). Since Harry is not a vertebrate, he must be an invertebrate (theorem `invert_Harry`).
- The antecedent of the conditional is true: Harry is neither a vertebrate nor has a backbone (theorem `antecedent_true`).
- The consequent claims Harry is neither an invertebrate nor a queen bee. But he is an invertebrate, making this false (theorem `consequent_false`).

Since the antecedent is true and the consequent is false, the entire conditional is false. The Lean code formalizes this reasoning and proves the conditional false.
