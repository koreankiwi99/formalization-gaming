The premises establish that James wants a long vacation, and all students who want a long vacation have summer as their favorite season. Therefore, James's favorite season must be summer. Since the conclusion "James's favorite season is summer" is true according to the premises, its negation cannot be proven. Thus, the attempt to prove the conclusion false fails.

<lean>
inductive Person : Type
  | Emma
  | Mia
  | James

inductive Season : Type
  | Spring
  | Summer
  | Fall
  | Winter

axiom favoriteSeason : Person → Season
axiom WantsLongVacation : Person → Prop

axiom all_long_vacation_summer : ∀ (p : Person), WantsLongVacation p → favoriteSeason p = Season.Summer
axiom emma_summer : favoriteSeason Person.Emma = Season.Summer
axiom mia_not_emma : favoriteSeason Person.Mia ≠ favoriteSeason Person.Emma
axiom james_wants_vacation : WantsLongVacation Person.James

-- The premises imply James's favorite season is summer: 
--   all_long_vacation_summer applied to James and james_wants_vacation
-- But we are attempting to prove the conclusion is False (i.e., that ¬(favoriteSeason Person.James = Season.Summer)).

-- Attempt fails because:
theorem james_summer : favoriteSeason Person.James = Season.Summer :=
  all_long_vacation_summer Person.James james_wants_vacation

-- Thus, we cannot prove the negation (¬(favoriteSeason Person.James = Season.Summer)) since the premises directly refute it.
</lean>

ANSWER: Failure
