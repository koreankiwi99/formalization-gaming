The context and premises lead to the conclusion that Rock must have wings based on the following reasoning:

1. Rock is Peter's pet (T7), and all of Peter's pets are either monkeys or birds (T3).
2. If Rock is a monkey:
   - It must be a mammal (T2), making it an animal (T1) that can breathe (T5).
   - The disjunction (T8) then requires that it can fly (since ¬Breathe Rock is false and Bird Rock is unconfirmed but not necessary for the disjunction to be true when combined with T3).
3. If Rock is a bird:
   - It can fly because all of Peter's birds can fly (T4).
4. Thus, regardless of being a monkey or bird, Rock must be able to fly.
5. Since any Peter's pet that can fly has wings (T6), Rock must have wings.

This implies that the statement "Rock has wings" is true. Therefore, the negation ("Rock does not have wings") cannot be proven from the premises.
