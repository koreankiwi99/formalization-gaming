The goal is to prove that the statement "If roses are given out during Breaking Bad, then it is on Monday at 8 pm" is false, which means proving its negation: "Roses are given out during Breaking Bad and it is not on Monday at 8 pm." However, the premises imply that Breaking Bad cannot give out roses at all. If Breaking Bad gave out roses, it would be an episode of The Bachelor (by R1), then portray real people (by R2), and thus be a reality TV show (by R3). But Breaking Bad is explicitly not a reality TV show (T4). This contradiction proves that Breaking Bad does not give out roses. Therefore, the antecedent "roses are given out during Breaking Bad" is false, making the entire implication vacuously true. Consequently, the negation of the implication cannot be proven.

axiom obj : Type
axiom BreakingBad : obj
axiom AiringAt8PMMonday : obj → Prop
axiom GivesOutRoses : obj → Prop
axiom EpisodeOfBachelor : obj → Prop
axiom PortraysRealPeople : obj → Prop
axiom RealityTV : obj → Prop

axiom T1 : ∃ s : obj, AiringAt8PMMonday s ∧ GivesOutRoses s
axiom R1 : ∀ s : obj, GivesOutRoses s → EpisodeOfBachelor s
axiom R2 : ∀ s : obj, EpisodeOfBachelor s → PortraysRealPeople s
axiom R3 : ∀ s : obj, PortraysRealPeople s → RealityTV s
axiom T4 : ¬ RealityTV BreakingBad

-- Proving that Breaking Bad cannot give out roses
theorem no_roses : ¬ GivesOutRoses BreakingBad :=
  fun hRoses : GivesOutRoses BreakingBad =>
    have hEp : EpisodeOfBachelor BreakingBad := R1 BreakingBad hRoses
    have hPortray : PortraysRealPeople BreakingBad := R2 BreakingBad hEp
    have hReality : RealityTV BreakingBad := R3 BreakingBad hPortray
    T4 hReality

-- The negation of the implication requires: GivesOutRoses BreakingBad ∧ ¬AiringAt8PMMonday BreakingBad
-- Since we have ¬GivesOutRoses BreakingBad (by no_roses), this conjunction cannot be proven.
