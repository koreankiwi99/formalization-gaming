The reasoning involves determining if "Breaking Bad is a TV show in which roses are given out on Monday at 8 pm" is false based on the given premises. The key steps are:

1. **Premises Analysis**:
   - If any show gives out roses, it must be an episode of The Bachelor (Premise 2).
   - The Bachelor portrays real lives (Premise 3), and such shows are reality TV (Premise 4).
   - Breaking Bad is not a reality TV show (Premise 6).

2. **Contradiction Detection**:
   - If Breaking Bad gave out roses (as claimed in the statement), it would imply Breaking Bad is an episode of The Bachelor (Premise 2).
   - This would require Breaking Bad to portray real lives (Premise 3) and thus be a reality TV show (Premise 4).
   - However, Premise 6 explicitly states Breaking Bad is not a reality TV show, creating a contradiction.

4. **Irrelevance of Airing Time**:
   - The airing time (Monday at 8 pm) is part of the statement, but the contradiction arises solely from the act of giving roses. Since the logical inconsistency exists regardless of airing time, the entire statement must be false.

axiom TVShow : Type
axiom BreakingBad : TVShow
axiom RosesGiven : TVShow → Prop
axiom AirsMonday8pm : TVShow → Prop
axiom IsBachelor : TVShow → Prop
axiom PortraysRealLives : TVShow → Prop
axiom RealityTV : TVShow → Prop

axiom roses_imply_bachelor : ∀ s : TVShow, RosesGiven s → IsBachelor s
axiom bachelor_real : ∀ s : TVShow, IsBachelor s → PortraysRealLives s
axiom real_implies_reality : ∀ s : TVShow, PortraysRealLives s → RealityTV s
axiom breakingbad_not_reality : ¬ RealityTV BreakingBad

theorem contradiction : False := 
  have stmt : AirsMonday8pm BreakingBad ∧ RosesGiven BreakingBad := by 
    sorry -- Assume the statement is correct for contradiction
  have roses : RosesGiven BreakingBad := stmt.right
  have bachelor : IsBachelor BreakingBad := roses_imply_bachelor _ roses
  have real_lives : PortraysRealLives BreakingBad := bachelor_real _ bachelor
