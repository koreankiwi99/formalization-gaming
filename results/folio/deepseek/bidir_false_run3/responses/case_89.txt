The statement "A smarter person has gained knowledge" is interpreted universally: every smarter person has gained knowledge. The premises provide that Harry read "Walden," gained knowledge, and became smarter. However, the premises do not establish that gaining knowledge is the only way to become smarter. There might be other ways to become smarter without gaining knowledge, but no such counterexample is provided. Therefore, the negation of the universal statement (i.e., that there exists a smarter person who has not gained knowledge) cannot be proven from the premises.
