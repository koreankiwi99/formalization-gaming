We attempted to prove that "KiKi is terricolous" is false (i.e., ¬Terricolous KiKi). However, after formalizing the premises, we can derive that if KiKi is terricolous, then it has legs, jumps, and Jane likes KiKi. But no premise forces Jane to dislike KiKi (since we lack evidence that KiKi barks). The special premise about KiKi doesn't produce a contradiction. Hence, we cannot prove the negation.
