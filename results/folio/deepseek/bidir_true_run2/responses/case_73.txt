The context provides information about vehicle registration plates and their relation to Istanbul and the starting numbers 34 and 35. The key premises are:

1. All Istanbul plates begin with 34 (∀p, Istanbul p → BeginsWith34 p)
2. Joe's plate is from Istanbul (Istanbul joesPlate)
3. Tom's plate begins with 35 (BeginsWith35 tomsPlate)
4. Plates starting with 35 cannot start with 34 (∀p, BeginsWith35 p → ¬BeginsWith34 p)

We need to prove: "Joe's license plate begins with 34".

**Proof:**
- From premise 1, anything from Istanbul has plate 34
- From premise 2, Joe's plate is from Istanbul
- Therefore, by universal instantiation and modus ponens, Joe's plate begins with 34

Tom's plate information and the 35/34 exclusion are not needed for this conclusion.

<lean>
axiom Plate : Type
axiom joesPlate : Plate
axiom tomsPlate : Plate
axiom BeginsWith34 : Plate → Prop
axiom BeginsWith35 : Plate → Prop
axiom FromIstanbul : Plate → Prop

axiom AllIstanbulPlatesBeginWith34 : ∀ (p : Plate), FromIstanbul p → BeginsWith34 p
axiom JoesPlateIsFromIstanbul : FromIstanbul joesPlate
axiom TomsPlateBeginsWith35 : BeginsWith35 tomsPlate
axiom BeginsWith35Not34 : ∀ (p : Plate), BeginsWith35 p → ¬ BeginsWith34 p

theorem JoesPlateBeginsWith34 : BeginsWith34 joesPlate := 
  AllIstanbulPlatesBeginWith34 joesPlate JoesPlateIsFromIstanbul
</lean>

ANSWER: True
