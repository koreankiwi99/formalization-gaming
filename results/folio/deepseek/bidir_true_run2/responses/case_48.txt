The premises establish relationships between digital media, analog media, printed text, streaming services, and hardcover books. Specifically:

- No digital media are analog (a contradiction if something is both).
- Printed texts are analog media.
- Streaming services are digital media.
- Hardcover books are printed texts.
- If 1984 is a streaming service, it must be a hardcover book.

The conclusion claims "1984 is printed text." To prove this:

1. **Incompatible properties**: If 1984 were a streaming service, it would be digital (premise 3). Digital media cannot be analog (premise 1). But if 1984 were a hardcover book (implied by premise 5), it would be printed text (premise 4) and thus analog (premise 2)—contradicting no digital media can be analog.  
   ⇒ **1984 cannot be a streaming service** (to avoid contradiction).  

2. **No path to the conclusion**: Without 1984 being a streaming service, premise 5 (an implication) doesn't activate. The axioms don't require 1984 to be a hardcover book or printed text. There are no premises forcing 1984 to be printed text directly or indirectly.  

Thus, the truth of "1984 is printed text" **cannot be proven** from the given premises.
