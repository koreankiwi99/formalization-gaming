The statement "Barutin Cove is named after all islands in Antarctica" requires proving that for every island located in Antarctica, Barutin Cove is named after it. The negation of this statement is that there exists an island in Antarctica that Barutin Cove is not named after.

From the premises:
- Barutin Cove is named after the Bulgarian settlement of Barutin.
- Barutin Cove is on Snow Island.
- Snow Island, Greenwich Island, and Deception Island are islands located in the South Shetland Islands.
- Antarctica is located on the South Shetland Islands.
- Transitivity applies for "located in" but not explicitly for "located on".

Key obstacles to proving the negation:
1. **Location ambiguity**: "Located on" is used inconsistently with "located in", and the transitivity axiom only applies to "located in". There is no axiom linking "located on" to "located in".
2. **Islands in Antarctica**: No premise establishes that any specific island (e.g., Snow Island) is located *in* Antarctica. Antarctica being "located on" the South Shetland Islands does not imply that islands in the South Shetland Islands are in Antarctica.
3. **Naming constraints**: While Barutin Cove is named after a settlement, there is no premise stating that this naming excludes islands or that any island is explicitly not named after Barutin Cove.

Thus, it is impossible to construct an island in Antarctica that Barutin Cove is not named after, as:
- No island is provably located in Antarctica.
- No contradiction exists between the naming of Barutin Cove and islands.

Therefore, the negation cannot be proven.
