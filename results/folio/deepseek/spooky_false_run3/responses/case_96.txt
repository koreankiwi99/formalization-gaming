The premises establish a hierarchy: private companies are companies, which are business organizations, which are legal entities. Legal entities are created under law and have legal obligations. The critical premise states that if the Harvard Weekly Book Club (HWBC) is created under law, it is not a private company.

**Proof structure:**  
1. Suppose HWBC is a private company (assumption for contradiction).  
2. Then HWBC is a legal entity (via: private company → company → business organization → legal entity).  
3. Thus HWBC is created under law (all legal entities are).  
4. Therefore, HWBC cannot be a private company (by the critical premise).  
5. Contradiction (between steps 1 and 4).  

This proves HWBC cannot be both a legal entity (with obligations) and a private company. The original statement is false.

<lean>
axiom Entity : Type
axiom hwbc : Entity

axiom LegalEntity : Entity → Prop
axiom BusinessOrganization : Entity → Prop
axiom Company : Entity → Prop
axiom PrivateCompany : Entity → Prop
axiom CreatedUnderLaw : Entity → Prop
axiom HasLegalObligations : Entity → Prop

axiom A1 : ∀ x : Entity, BusinessOrganization x → LegalEntity x
axiom A2 : ∀ x : Entity, Company x → BusinessOrganization x
axiom A3 : ∀ x : Entity, PrivateCompany x → Company x
axiom A4 : ∀ x : Entity, LegalEntity x → CreatedUnderLaw x
axiom A5 : ∀ x : Entity, LegalEntity x → HasLegalObligations x
axiom A6 : CreatedUnderLaw hwbc → ¬ PrivateCompany hwbc

theorem disprove : ¬ (HasLegalObligations hwbc ∧ PrivateCompany hwbc) := by
  intro ⟨_, h_pc⟩
  have h_cmp : Company hwbc := A3 _ h_pc
  have h_bo : BusinessOrganization hwbc := A2 _ h_cmp
  have h_le : LegalEntity hwbc := A1 _ h_bo
  have h_cul : CreatedUnderLaw hwbc := A4 _ h_le
  exact A6 h_cul h_pc
</lean>

ANSWER: False
