The natural language reasoning is translated into Lean axioms. The goal is to prove that the conclusion "Rock has wings" is false (i.e., ¬HasWings Rock). However, after analyzing the premises:

1. Rock is Peter's pet (A7).
2. By A3, Rock is either a monkey or a bird.
3. If Rock is a bird (A4), then Rock can fly, and by A6, Rock has wings.
4. If Rock is a monkey (A2), then Rock is a mammal, and by A1, Rock is an animal. Then by A5, Rock can breathe. With A8, since Rock can breathe and is not a bird (as monkeys and birds are distinct), Rock must be able to fly. By A6, Rock has wings.
5. In both cases (monkey or bird), Rock has wings.

Thus, the premises entail that Rock has wings (HasWings Rock). Therefore, it is impossible to prove the negation (¬HasWings Rock). The attempt to prove the conclusion false fails.
