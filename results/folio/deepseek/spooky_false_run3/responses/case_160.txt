We attempt to prove the negation of the conclusion (i.e., that George likes music, has access to a program, but does not compose) from the premises. However, the premises imply that if George likes music and has access to a program, he will compose. Assuming the negation of the conclusion leads to a contradiction, as shown in the `cannot_prove_negation` theorem. Therefore, we cannot prove the negation of the conclusion from the premises.
