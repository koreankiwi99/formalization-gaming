The premises state:
1. Susan flies to LGA (arrives at LGA).
2. Departure and arrival cannot be at the same airport for any flight.
3. John flies from LGA (departs from LGA).

The statement to evaluate is: "Susan flies from LGA airport." To prove its negation, assume Susan departs from LGA. Since Susan arrives at LGA (premise 1), this would mean her departure and arrival are both at LGA, violating premise 2 (which forbids departure and arrival at the same airport). This contradiction proves the statement is false.

<lean>
axiom Airport : Type
axiom Person : Type
axiom LGA : Airport
axiom Susan : Person
axiom John : Person
axiom Departure : Person → Airport → Prop
axiom Arrival : Person → Airport → Prop

axiom SusanArrivesLGA : Arrival Susan LGA
axiom NoSameAirport : ∀ (p : Person) (a : Airport), Departure p a → Arrival p a → False
axiom JohnDepartsLGA : Departure John LGA

theorem SusanNotFromLGA : ¬Departure Susan LGA :=
  fun h : Departure Susan LGA => 
    NoSameAirport Susan LGA h SusanArrivesLGA

</lean>

ANSWER: False
