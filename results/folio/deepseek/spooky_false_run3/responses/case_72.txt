The goal is to prove that the given statement is false based on the premises. The statement is: "If KO is a growth stock or bought to earn profits from rapid price appreciation, then KO is neither a stock nor is its price volatile." 

After formalizing the premises in Lean, we derive:
- KO is a mature stock (premise 5).
- All mature stocks are suitable for retirement (premise 4), so KO is suitable for retirement.
- If KO were a growth stock, it would be bought for profit (premise 1), making it unsuitable for retirement (premise 2), contradicting the previous point. Thus, KO is not a growth stock.
- If KO were bought for profit, it would be unsuitable for retirement (premise 2), again contradicting KO's suitability. Thus, KO is not bought for profit.
- The antecedent (KO is a growth stock or bought for profit) is false.
- The implication (false → anything) is true, regardless of the consequent about KO being a stock or volatility.

Since the statement is true, we cannot prove it false. The premises do not provide information about volatility or require mature stocks to be stocks, so the consequent's truth value cannot be fully determined, but the antecedent's falsity makes the whole statement true.
