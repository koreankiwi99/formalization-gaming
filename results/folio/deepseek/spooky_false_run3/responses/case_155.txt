However, the conclusion "Some Greeks are mortal" is an existential claim requiring at least one Greek to exist. The premises do not assert the existence of any Greek. Therefore, while the premises guarantee that *if* there are Greeks, they are mortal, they do not prove that there are no Greeks (which would make the conclusion false). Consequently, the negation of the conclusion cannot be proven from the premises.
