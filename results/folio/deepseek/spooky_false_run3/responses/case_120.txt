The premises state that some affection is love and some love is positive. However, these do not necessitate that some affection is positive. The affection that is love might not be positive, and the love that is positive might not be affection. Therefore, it is impossible to prove that no affection is positive (the negation of the conclusion) from the given premises.

<lean>
axiom obj : Type
axiom Affection : obj → Prop
axiom Love : obj → Prop
axiom Positive : obj → Prop

axiom P1 : ∃ x, Affection x ∧ Love x
axiom P2 : ∃ x, Love x ∧ Positive x

theorem disprove : ¬ (∃ x, Affection x ∧ Positive x) :=
  fun ⟨w, hAff, hPos⟩ => 
    -- We have an object w that is both Affection and Positive.
    -- However, P1 and P2 only guarantee separate objects with Affection-Love and Love-Positive properties.
    -- There is no premise linking Affection directly to Positive, nor any contradiction introduced.
    -- Thus, no contradiction can be derived from the existence of w.
    -- The premises do not constrain whether the affection (from P1) is positive or if the positive love (from P2) is affection.
    -- Therefore, the negation cannot be proven.
    by admit
</lean>
