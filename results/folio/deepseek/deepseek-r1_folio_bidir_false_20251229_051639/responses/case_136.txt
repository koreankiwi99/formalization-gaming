The given statement is "If something is a household appliance, it sucks." This can be translated as the universal claim: ∀ x, HouseholdAppliance x → Sucks x.

However, the context provides a direct counterexample: "A duster is a household appliance that doesn't suck." This establishes:
- There exists a duster (a specific object).
- The duster is a household appliance.
- The duster does not suck.

This contradicts the universal statement, as we have an instance where the premise (being a household appliance) is true but the conclusion (sucking) is false. Therefore, the universal claim is false.

The additional context (about plungers, vacuums, vampires, and space) does not affect the counterexample provided by the duster, making it sufficient for proving the statement false.
