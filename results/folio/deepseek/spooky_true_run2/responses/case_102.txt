<lean>
axiom Person : Type
axiom Luke : Person

axiom BornMultiple : Person → Prop
axiom HasSiblingsTogether : Person → Prop
axiom ComplainsOften : Person → Prop
axiom SpendTimeWithSiblings : Person → Prop
axiom LiveAtHome : Person → Prop
axiom LiveWithStrangers : Person → Prop

-- Premises from context:
axiom P1 : ∀ p : Person, BornMultiple p → SpendTimeWithSiblings p
axiom P2 : ∀ p : Person, HasSiblingsTogether p → BornMultiple p
axiom P3 : ∀ p : Person, ComplainsOften p → HasSiblingsTogether p
axiom P4 : ∀ p : Person, LiveAtHome p → ¬ LiveWithStrangers p
axiom P5 : ∀ p : Person, SpendTimeWithSiblings p → LiveAtHome p
axiom LukeDisjunction : 
    (BornMultiple Luke ∧ LiveWithStrangers Luke) ∨ 
    (¬ BornMultiple Luke ∧ ¬ LiveWithStrangers Luke)

-- Attempt to prove Luke complains often
theorem luke_complains : ComplainsOften Luke := by
  -- Analyze the disjunction
  cases LukeDisjunction with
  | inl h_case1 =>  -- Case 1: BornMultiple and LiveWithStrangers
    -- Derive consequences from premises
    have spend_time : SpendTimeWithSiblings Luke := P1 Luke h_case1.1
    have live_home : LiveAtHome Luke := P5 Luke spend_time
    have no_strangers : ¬ LiveWithStrangers Luke := P4 Luke live_home
    -- Contradiction: h_case1.2 (lives with strangers) contradicts no_strangers
    contradiction
  | inr h_case2 =>  -- Case 2: Not BornMultiple and Not LiveWithStrangers
    -- Assuming Luke complains often to derive contradiction
    by_contra h_complains
    have siblings_together : HasSiblingsTogether Luke := P3 Luke h_complains
    have born_multi : BornMultiple Luke := P2 Luke siblings_together
    -- Contradiction: born_multi contradicts h_case2.1 (not BornMultiple)
    exact h_case2.1 born_multi
</lean>

In the proof attempt:
1. **Case 1 (Luke is born in a multiple birth and lives with strangers)** leads to a contradiction:
   - `P1` implies Luke spends time with siblings; `P5` implies he lives at home.
   - `P4` then implies he doesn't live with strangers, contradicting the case's premise.
2. **Case 2 (Luke is not born in a multiple birth and doesn't live with strangers)** also exposes issues when we assume he complains:
   - `P3` and `P2` would force Luke to be born in a multiple birth, contradicting the case.
   - The proof by contradiction (`by_contra`) fails to establish `ComplainsOften Luke`, as the assumption leads to logical inconsistency.

Since Case 1 is impossible due to contradictions in the premises, Case 2 must hold. However, Case 2 **refutes** the statement that Luke complains, making `ComplainsOften Luke` unprovable from the premises.
