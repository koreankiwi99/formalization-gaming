The natural language statements provide:
- "Symphony No. 9 is a music piece" → `MusicPiece Symphony9`
- "Vienna Music Society premiered Symphony No. 9" → `Premiere ViennaMusicSociety Symphony9`
- "Vienna Music Society is an orchestra" → `Orchestra ViennaMusicSociety`
These directly prove "Some orchestras premiered music pieces" by exhibiting `ViennaMusicSociety` as an orchestra that premiered the music piece `Symphony9`. Other premises about composers, Beethoven, or leadership are irrelevant to the existential conclusion.

<lean>
axiom obj : Type

-- Constants
axiom Symphony9 : obj
axiom Beethoven : obj
axiom ViennaMusicSociety : obj

-- Predicates
axiom MusicPiece : obj → Prop
axiom Orchestra : obj → Prop
axiom Premiere : obj → obj → Prop

-- Premises
axiom P1 : MusicPiece Symphony9
axiom P3 : Premiere ViennaMusicSociety Symphony9
axiom P4 : Orchestra ViennaMusicSociety

theorem some_orchestra_premiered : ∃ orchestra music, Orchestra orchestra ∧ MusicPiece music ∧ Premiere orchestra music :=
  by
    apply Exists.intro ViennaMusicSociety
    apply Exists.intro Symphony9
    constructor
    · exact P4
    · constructor
      · exact P1
      · exact P3

</lean>

ANSWER: True
