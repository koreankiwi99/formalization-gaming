    GREApplicant x →

theorem ets_aid_Tom : GetsFinancialAid Tom := by

  · exact gre_applicant_Tom

1. We know Tom is a GRE applicant (premise 4)
2. We know Tom lives in a single-parent family (premise 3)
3. The economic hardship rule states that either living in a single-parent family OR having few resources proves economic hardship
4. Since Tom satisfies the first condition (single-parent family), we prove economic hardship via left disjunction
5. The financial aid rule then applies since Tom is an applicant with proven economic hardship

The conclusion "ETS provides financial aid to Tom" follows necessarily from these premises. The cost comparison ($205 vs $300) is irrelevant to the logical deduction and Tom's dad's employment status is not required for the proof as the single-parent condition alone suffices.
