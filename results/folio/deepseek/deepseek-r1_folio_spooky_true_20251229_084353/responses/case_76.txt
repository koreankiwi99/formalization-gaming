The premises lead to a contradiction, making the conclusion provable through the principle of explosion (ex falso quodlibet). Specifically:
- Premises establish that Hannah works on campus (P7).
- Premise 1 implies Hannah needs money (P1 applied to Hannah).
- The second part of premise 7 states that if Hannah needs money, she does not need money (and is not picky).
- This creates a contradiction: Hannah both needs money (from P1) and does not need money (from P7 and P1).
- From this contradiction, any statement follows, including the conclusion.

<lean>
axiom obj : Type
axiom Hannah : obj

axiom AtMarySchool : obj → Prop
axiom WorksOnCampus : obj → Prop
axiom NeedsMoney : obj → Prop
axiom OrdersTakeout : obj → Prop
axiom EnjoysDining : obj → Prop
axiom PickyEater : obj → Prop
axiom SpendsTimeEating : obj → Prop

axiom P1 : ∀ p, AtMarySchool p → WorksOnCampus p → NeedsMoney p
axiom P2 : ∀ p, AtMarySchool p → OrdersTakeout p → WorksOnCampus p
axiom P3 : ∀ p, AtMarySchool p → OrdersTakeout p ∨ EnjoysDining p
axiom P4 : ∀ p, AtMarySchool p → EnjoysDining p → ¬ PickyEater p
axiom P5 : ∀ p, AtMarySchool p → EnjoysDining p → SpendsTimeEating p
axiom P6 : AtMarySchool Hannah
axiom P7 : WorksOnCampus Hannah ∧ (NeedsMoney Hannah → (¬ PickyEater Hannah ∧ ¬ NeedsMoney Hannah))

theorem target : AtMarySchool Hannah ∧ ¬ PickyEater Hannah ∧ SpendsTimeEating Hannah := by
  have a1 : NeedsMoney Hannah := P1 Hannah P6 P7.1
  have a2 := P7.2 a1
  exact False.elim (a2.2 a1)
</lean>

ANSWER: True
