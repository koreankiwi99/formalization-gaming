The given context includes several axioms, but the key issue is determining whether James might be in other countries, which would affect the truth of the statement. The logical statement in question is:  
- If James is a manager or in other countries, then he does not have lunch at home or work remotely.  
- If he has lunch at home or works remotely, then he is not a manager and not in other countries.  

While the manager case works (managers cannot work remotely and must lunch in the building, satisfying the conclusion), the case where James is in other countries causes a problem:  
- If James is in other countries, he must work remotely (axiom 5).  
- This makes the conjunction (has lunch at home ∨ works remotely) true.  
- But the conclusion requires this conjunction to be *false* when James is in other countries (premise true).  

The context does **not** provide:  
1. An explicit link between being in other countries and being unable to lunch in the building/go to the building.  
2. Information about James's actual location or manager status.  

Thus, if James is in other countries, the implication fails, but the context does not force him to be in other countries. The statement holds for managers, but without knowing James's status, we cannot universally prove it. The negation also cannot be proven, as nobody forces James to be in other countries. Therefore, neither the statement nor its negation is provable from the axioms.
