4. Valuing order and spotlessness implies being cleanly.
5. If a family prioritizes order and spotlessness, the person values it.

The case where Peter spills food and is cleanly leads to a contradiction: spilling food implies not notably tidy (premise 1), but being cleanly implies notably tidy (premise 3). Hence, we cannot prove that Peter is notably tidy.
