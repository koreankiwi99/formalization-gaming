The statement "Robert Lewandowski is a soccer player" directly follows from the first two premises:  
1. Robert Lewandowski is a striker.  
2. Strikers are soccer players (i.e., all strikers are soccer players).  

The additional premises about leaving Bayern Munchen and the rule for leaving a team do not affect the conclusion, as they pertain to team membership rather than the player's general status as a soccer player.  

Therefore, the conclusion is provable using only the relevant premises.

<lean>
axiom obj : Type
axiom RobertLewandowski : obj
axiom Striker : obj → Prop
axiom SoccerPlayer : obj → Prop
axiom T1 : Striker RobertLewandowski
axiom R1 : ∀ x : obj, Striker x → SoccerPlayer x
theorem lewandowski_soccer_player : SoccerPlayer RobertLewandowski := 
  R1 RobertLewandowski T1
</lean>

ANSWER: True
