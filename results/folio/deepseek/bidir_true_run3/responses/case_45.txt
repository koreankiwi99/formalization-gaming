The context states that all cats are mammals and some pets are not mammals. The statement to evaluate is that no pets are cats. 

In Lean, we represent:
- `obj` as the type of objects
- `Cat`, `Mammal`, and `Pet` as predicates on objects
- Axiom 1: All cats are mammals (`∀ x, Cat x → Mammal x`)
- Axiom 2: There exists a pet that is not a mammal (`∃ x, Pet x ∧ ¬ Mammal x`)

The conclusion "no pets are cats" means that for all objects, if it is a pet then it is not a cat (`∀ x, Pet x → ¬ Cat x`). 

However, from the given axioms:
- Axiom 1 tells us that cats are mammals, but says nothing about pets.
- Axiom 2 tells us there is at least one pet that is not a mammal, but does not preclude the existence of pets that are cats (as long as those cats are mammals, which is consistent with Axiom 1).

There is no contradiction in assuming a pet that is a cat: such a pet would be a mammal (by Axiom 1), which does not violate Axiom 2 (which only requires one non-mammal pet). Therefore, the conclusion cannot be proven from the premises.
