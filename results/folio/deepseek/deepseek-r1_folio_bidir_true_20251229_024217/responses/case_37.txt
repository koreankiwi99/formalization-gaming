- Symphony No. 9 is a music piece.
- Beethoven wrote Symphony No. 9, but this fact is not required for the proof.
- Vienna Music Society premiered Symphony No. 9.
- Vienna Music Society is an orchestra.
- Additional facts about Beethoven leading the orchestra and orchestras being led by conductors are not needed to prove the target statement.

The target statement requires showing that there exists at least one orchestra that premiered at least one music piece. Specifically, Vienna Music Society is an orchestra and it premiered Symphony No. 9, which is a music piece. This provides a concrete witness for the existential claim.

Lean proof:
- We define the type `obj` to represent entities.
- Constants `Symphony_No_9` and `Vienna_Music_Society` represent specific objects.
- Predicates are defined for properties and relations:
  - `MusicPiece (x)` for "x is a music piece".
  - `Orchestra (x)` for "x is an orchestra".
  - `Premiered (x y)` for "x premiered y".
- Axioms assert:
  - Symphony No. 9 is a music piece (`T1`).
  - Vienna Music Society premiered Symphony No. 9 (`T2`).
  - Vienna Music Society is an orchestra (`T3`).
- The theorem `some_orchestra` proves the statement by providing `Vienna_Music_Society` and `Symphony_No_9` as witnesses.

axiom obj : Type
axiom Symphony_No_9 : obj
axiom Vienna_Music_Society : obj

theorem some_orchestra : ∃ x, ∃ y, Orchestra x ∧ MusicPiece y ∧ Premiered x y :=
