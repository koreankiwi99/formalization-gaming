The reasoning problem is translated into Lean by formalizing the entities and predicates. The type `obj` represents individuals. The predicates are:
- `NiceToAnimals`: an individual is nice to animals.
- `MeanToAnimals`: an individual is mean to animals.
- `GrumpyPerson`: an individual is grumpy.
- `AnimalLover`: an individual loves animals.
- `PetOwner`: an individual owns pets.
- `Tom` is a specific constant of type `obj`.

**Axioms from the context:**
1. `A1`: No one is both nice and mean to animals (∀ x, `NiceToAnimals x → ¬ MeanToAnimals x`).
2. `A2`: Some grumpy people are mean to animals (∃ x, `GrumpyPerson x ∧ MeanToAnimals x`). Note: This existential is not needed for Tom's proof.
3. `A3`: All animal lovers are nice to animals (∀ x, `AnimalLover x → NiceToAnimals x`).
4. `A4`: All pet owners love animals (∀ x, `PetOwner x → AnimalLover x`).
5. `A5`: Tom is a pet owner (`PetOwner Tom`).

**Conclusion to prove:**  
"Tom is not both a grumpy person and mean to animals" translates to `¬(GrumpyPerson Tom ∧ MeanToAnimals Tom)`.

**Proof Sketch:**  
From `A5` (Tom is a pet owner) and `A4` (pet owners love animals), Tom is an animal lover (`AnimalLover Tom`). By `A3` (animal lovers are nice to animals), Tom is nice to animals (`NiceToAnimals Tom`). By `A1` (nice to animals implies not mean to animals), Tom is not mean to animals (`¬ MeanToAnimals Tom`). Thus, if Tom were grumpy and mean, we would have a contradiction.

**Lean Implementation:**  
<lean>
axiom obj : Type
axiom Tom : obj
axiom NiceToAnimals : obj → Prop
axiom MeanToAnimals : obj → Prop
axiom GrumpyPerson : obj → Prop
axiom AnimalLover : obj → Prop
axiom PetOwner : obj → Prop

axiom A1 : ∀ x, NiceToAnimals x → ¬ MeanToAnimals x
axiom A2 : ∃ x, GrumpyPerson x ∧ MeanToAnimals x
axiom A3 : ∀ x, AnimalLover x → NiceToAnimals x

axiom A5 : PetOwner Tom

theorem Tom_not_grumpy_and_mean : ¬ (GrumpyPerson Tom ∧ MeanToAnimals Tom) :=
  fun ⟨grumpy, mean⟩ => 
    have lover : AnimalLover Tom := A4 Tom A5
    have nice : NiceToAnimals Tom := A3 Tom lover
    have not_mean : ¬ MeanToAnimals Tom := A1 Tom nice
    not_mean mean
</lean>

The proof establishes the conclusion using axioms `A5`, `A4`, `A3`, and `A1`. Axiom `A2` (about some grumpy person) is not required for Tom's case. Since the proof is constructive, the answer is **True**.
