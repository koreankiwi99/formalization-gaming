1. All hydrocarbons are organic compounds.
2. All alkanes are hydrocarbons.
3. All organic compounds are chemical compounds.
4. All organic compounds contain carbon.
5. No chemical compounds contain only one element.
6. The mixture is either both a chemical compound and contains only one element, or neither.

The key derivation:
- Premise 5 and 6 imply the mixture cannot be both a chemical compound and have only one element.
- Thus, the mixture is neither a chemical compound nor has only one element (`Mixture_fixed`).
- The statement's conclusion (`¬CC Mixture ∧ ¬Alk Mixture`) always holds:
  - `¬CC Mixture` comes directly from `Mixture_fixed`.
  - `¬Alk Mixture` is proven by contradiction: if it were an alkane, it would be a chemical compound (via hydrocarbons and organic compounds), contradicting `¬CC Mixture`.
- Since the conclusion holds regardless of the antecedent, the implication is true.

theorem target :
