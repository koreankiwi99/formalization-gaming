The reasoning involves translating the given context and Luke's situation into logical predicates and axioms, then proving the statement that Luke is neither born in a multiple birth nor complains often about annoying siblings.

- **Premise 1**: Born in a multiple birth → Spends time with siblings (A1)
- **Premise 2**: Spends time with siblings → Lives at home (A5)
- **Premise 3**: Lives at home → Does not live with strangers (A4)
- **Premise 4**: Complains about siblings → Has siblings born together (A3)
- **Premise 5**: Has siblings born together → Born in a multiple birth (A2)
- **Luke's Case**: (Born multiple ∧ Lives with strangers) ∨ (¬Born multiple ∧ ¬Lives with strangers)

**Proof**:
1. Combining premises 1–3: If born in a multiple birth, then lives at home (contrapositively: lives with strangers → not born in multiple birth).
2. Luke's case: The first subcase (Born multiple ∧ Lives with strangers) contradicts the above chain (since lives with strangers implies ¬born multiple).
3. Thus, only the second subcase holds: Luke is not born in a multiple birth and does not live with strangers.
4. Now, if Luke complains about siblings (Premise 4), he must have siblings born together (Premise 5), implying he was born in a multiple birth. But this contradicts step 3.
5. Hence, Luke cannot complain about siblings.

Therefore, the statement is **provable** (True).
