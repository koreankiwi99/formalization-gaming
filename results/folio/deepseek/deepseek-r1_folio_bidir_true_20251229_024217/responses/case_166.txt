Based on the context, we know Heinrich Schmidt was German. The statement claims he was German or Russian or both. Since we have evidence he was German, the disjunction "German or Russian" must hold true regardless of his Russian status. Here's the Lean proof:

<lean>
axiom obj : Type
axiom HS : obj  -- Heinrich Schmidt
axiom German : obj → Prop
axiom Russian : obj → Prop
axiom german_HS : German HS  -- Premise: Heinrich Schmidt was German
theorem statement : German HS ∨ Russian HS := Or.inl german_HS
</lean>

ANSWER: True

Explanation:
- `Or.inl` proves `P ∨ Q` by providing evidence of `P`. Here, `german_HS` is proof of `German HS`, satisfying the disjunction.
- No information about Russian nationality exists in context, but German nationality alone suffices to prove the disjunction.
