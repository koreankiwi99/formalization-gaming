The reasoning problem involves determining if the statement "If the Harvard Weekly Book club is a private company, then it either has legal obligations or is created under law" follows from the given premises. After analysis using Lean, the statement is proven true based on the following:

1. If Harvard Weekly Book Club (HWBC) is a private company:
   - It is a company (from "All private companies are companies").
   - Therefore, it is a business organization (from "All companies are business organizations").
   - Hence, it is a legal entity (from "All business organizations are legal entities").
   - Thus, it has legal obligations (from "All legal entities have legal obligations").

2. Since it has legal obligations, the disjunction "either has legal obligations or is created under law" holds trivially (left disjunct is true).

The key observation:
- "Created under law" is not necessary to satisfy the disjunction once "has legal obligations" is established.
- The premise "If HWBC is created under law then it is not a private company" is not needed in the proof since the chain of implications (private company → legal entity → legal obligations) directly proves the consequent.

In Lean, we formalize the entities and axioms, then prove the theorem showing that assuming HWBC is a private company leads to it having legal obligations (via the disjunction's left side).

<lean>
axiom obj : Type
axiom HWBC : obj
axiom BusinessOrganization : obj → Prop
axiom LegalEntity : obj → Prop
axiom Company : obj → Prop
axiom PrivateCompany : obj → Prop
axiom CreatedUnderLaw : obj → Prop
axiom HasLegalObligations : obj → Prop

-- All business organizations are legal entities
axiom A1 : ∀ x, BusinessOrganization x → LegalEntity x

-- All companies are business organizations
axiom A2 : ∀ x, Company x → BusinessOrganization x

-- All private companies are companies
axiom A3 : ∀ x, PrivateCompany x → Company x

-- All legal entities are created under law - NOTE: Unused in proof.
axiom A4 : ∀ x, LegalEntity x → CreatedUnderLaw x

-- All legal entities have legal obligations
axiom A5 : ∀ x, LegalEntity x → HasLegalObligations x

-- If HWBC is created under law, it is not a private company - NOTE: Unused in proof.
axiom A6 : CreatedUnderLaw HWBC → ¬ PrivateCompany HWBC

theorem target : 
  PrivateCompany HWBC → 
  (HasLegalObligations HWBC ∨ CreatedUnderLaw HWBC) := 
  fun h_pc => 
    have h_company : Company HWBC := A3 HWBC h_pc
    have h_business : BusinessOrganization HWBC := A2 HWBC h_company
    have h_legal : LegalEntity HWBC := A1 HWBC h_business
    have h_obligations : HasLegalObligations HWBC := A5 HWBC h_legal
    Or.inl h_obligations
</lean>

ANSWER: True
