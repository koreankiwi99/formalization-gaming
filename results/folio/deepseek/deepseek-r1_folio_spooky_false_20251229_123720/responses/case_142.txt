The premises establish that breeding back is a form of artificial selection, Heck cattle underwent breeding back in the 1920s, and Heck cattle are animals. However, the premises do not specify that all Heck cattle (including present-day descendants) are bred back or artificially selected. While the existence of Heck cattle in the 1920s that were bred back (and thus artificially selected) can be proven, this does not guarantee that *current* Heck cattle are artificially selected. The statement "Some Heck cattle are artificially selected" could be true (due to past individuals) or false (if referring only to present individuals). The ambiguity prevents proving the negation.
