The premises lead to a contradiction when assuming "The Legend of Zelda is not on the Top 10 list." Specifically:
- Premise 3 states that any game selling over one million copies is on the Top 10 list.
- Premise 4 states that The Legend of Zelda sold over one million copies.
- Therefore, The Legend of Zelda must be on the Top 10 list (by Premise 3 and 4).
- Assuming it is not on the Top 10 list leads to a contradiction.

The Lean proof formalizes this reasoning:
- Defines the game entity and relevant predicates.
- Asserts the premises as axioms.
- Proves `OnTop10 zelda` directly from Premise 3 and 4.
- Proves that the negation of the statement (`¬OnTop10 zelda`) implies `False`.

<lean>
axiom obj : Type
axiom zelda : obj
axiom JapaneseMade : obj → Prop
axiom OnTop10 : obj → Prop
axiom SellsOver1M : obj → Prop

axiom P1 : JapaneseMade zelda
axiom P2 : ∀ g, OnTop10 g → JapaneseMade g
axiom P3 : ∀ g, SellsOver1M g → OnTop10 g
axiom P4 : SellsOver1M zelda

theorem zelda_on_top : OnTop10 zelda := P3 zelda P4

theorem false_from_neg_statement : ¬OnTop10 zelda → False := 
  fun h => h zelda_on_top
</lean>

ANSWER: False
