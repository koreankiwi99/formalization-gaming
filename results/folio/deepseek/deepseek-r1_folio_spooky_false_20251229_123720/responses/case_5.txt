The reasoning involves translating the premises into Lean and attempting to prove the negation of the given biconditional statement about James. The biconditional is:  
(manager James ∨ in_other_countries James) ↔ (¬ lunch_at_home James ∧ ¬ works_remotely_home James)

After formalizing the premises:
- Employees either have lunch in the building or at home (exclusive for James, derived from contradictions).
- If James has lunch in the building, he is a manager and not working remotely.
- If James has lunch at home, he works remotely and cannot be a manager.
- The biconditional holds when James is a manager (lunch in building) or when he is neither a manager nor in other countries (lunch at home without being abroad).
- However, the biconditional fails only if James is in other countries and has lunch at home, but the premises do not force this scenario. James could be not in other countries, making the biconditional true.

Since there exists a model (e.g., James is a manager) where the premises hold and the biconditional is true, its negation cannot be proven. Thus, the proof fails.

<lean>
axiom Employee : Type
axiom James : Employee

axiom schedules_meeting : Employee → Prop
axiom goes_to_building : Employee → Prop
axiom lunch_in_building : Employee → Prop
axiom lunch_at_home : Employee → Prop
axiom works_remotely_home : Employee → Prop
axiom in_other_countries : Employee → Prop
axiom manager : Employee → Prop

axiom P1 : ∀ e : Employee, schedules_meeting e → goes_to_building e
axiom P2 : ∀ e : Employee, lunch_in_building e → schedules_meeting e
axiom P3 : ∀ e : Employee, lunch_in_building e ∨ lunch_at_home e
axiom P4 : ∀ e : Employee, lunch_at_home e → works_remotely_home e
axiom P5 : ∀ e : Employee, in_other_countries e → works_remotely_home e
axiom P6 : ∀ e : Employee, manager e → ¬ works_remotely_home e
axiom P7 : goes_to_building James ↔ manager James

-- Prove James cannot have both lunch locations
theorem no_both : ¬ (lunch_in_building James ∧ lunch_at_home James) := by
  intro ⟨h_in, h_home⟩
  have h_sched := P2 James h_in
  have h_goes := P1 James h_sched
  have h_manager := P7.1 h_goes
  exact P6 James h_manager (P4 James h_home)

-- Attempt to prove the biconditional false (i.e., its negation)
theorem goal : ¬ ((manager James ∨ in_other_countries James) ↔ (¬ lunch_at_home James ∧ ¬ works_remotely_home James)) := by
  -- We cannot prove this negation since the biconditional can be true in some models
  -- Case: James is a manager (lunch in building) → biconditional holds
  -- Case: James not manager, not abroad, lunch at home → biconditional holds
  -- Thus, no contradiction in all models
  admit  -- Proof fails

</lean>
