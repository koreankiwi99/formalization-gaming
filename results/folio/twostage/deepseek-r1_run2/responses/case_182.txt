=== REASONING ===
We are given the following axioms and need to prove that Tom is not both grumpy and mean to animals.

Axioms:
P1: ∀ x, NiceToAnimals x → ¬ MeanToAnimals x
   (If someone is nice to animals, they are not mean to animals.)
P2: ∃ x, Grumpy x ∧ MeanToAnimals x
   (There exists a grumpy person who is mean to animals.)
P3: ∀ x, AnimalLover x → NiceToAnimals x
   (All animal lovers are nice to animals.)
P4: ∀ x, PetOwner x → AnimalLover x
   (All pet owners are animal lovers.)
P5: PetOwner Tom
   (Tom is a pet owner.)

Conclusion: ¬(Grumpy Tom ∧ MeanToAnimals Tom)

We can break down the proof as follows:

1. From P5 (Tom is a pet owner) and P4 (all pet owners are animal lovers), we get that Tom is an animal lover.
2. From being an animal lover (and P3), we get that Tom is nice to animals.
3. From being nice to animals (and P1), we get that Tom is not mean to animals (i.e., ¬ MeanToAnimals Tom).
4. Now, we need to show that Tom cannot be both grumpy and mean to animals. But we already have that Tom is not mean to animals. So, if someone claims that Tom is grumpy and mean to animals, that would require MeanToAnimals Tom, which contradicts our earlier conclusion.

Therefore, we can prove the theorem by assuming that Tom is both grumpy and mean to animals and then deriving a contradiction.

Proof in Lean:

We can write:
- Have `TomAnimalLover : AnimalLover Tom` from P5 and P4.
- Then `TomNice : NiceToAnimals Tom` from TomAnimalLover and P3.
- Then `TomNotMean : ¬ MeanToAnimals Tom` from TomNice and P1 (applied to Tom).

Now, to prove `¬(Grumpy Tom ∧ MeanToAnimals Tom)`:
  We assume `H : Grumpy Tom ∧ MeanToAnimals Tom`, and we need to show False.
  From `H` we get `MeanToAnimals Tom`.
  But we also have `TomNotMean` which is `¬ MeanToAnimals Tom`.
  Contradiction.

So we can write the proof as:

```lean
theorem conclusion : ¬(Grumpy Tom ∧ MeanToAnimals Tom) :=
  fun ⟨grumpyTom, meanTom⟩ =>
    have TomAnimalLover : AnimalLover Tom := P4 Tom P5
    have TomNice : NiceToAnimals Tom := P3 Tom TomAnimalLover
    have TomNotMean : ¬ MeanToAnimals Tom := P1 Tom TomNice
    TomNotMean meanTom
```

Alternatively, we can write it in a more concise way without naming the intermediate steps:

```lean
theorem conclusion : ¬(Grumpy Tom ∧ MeanToAnimals Tom) :=
  fun ⟨_, meanTom⟩ =>
    P1 Tom (P3 Tom (P4 Tom P5)) meanTom
```

But for clarity, we might prefer the first version. However, the short version works because:
- `P4 Tom P5` proves `AnimalLover Tom`
- `P3 Tom (P4 Tom P5)` proves `NiceToAnimals Tom`
- `P1 Tom (P3 Tom (P4 Tom P5))` proves `¬ MeanToAnimals Tom`

Then we get the `meanTom` (which is a proof of `MeanToAnimals Tom`) and apply the negation to get a contradiction.
