=== RESPONSE ===
The conclusion "If George will not compose, George can not compose" is not entailed by the premises. Here's why:

1. The premises establish:
   - If George likes music, he wants to compose
   - If he has access to a program, he can compose
   - If he can compose AND wants to compose, he will compose

2. The conclusion requires that not composing implies he cannot compose

3. Counterexample:
   - Suppose George has access to a program (so he can compose)
   - But he doesn't like music (so by P1, we don't know if he wants to compose)
   - If he doesn't want to compose, then even though he can compose, he won't compose (by P3's condition failing)
   - Here, ¬WillCompose is true but ¬CanCompose is false

Since we found a counterexample where the premises are true but the conclusion is false, the argument is invalid.
