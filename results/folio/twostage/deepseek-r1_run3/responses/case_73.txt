Premises:
1. All vehicle registration plates in Istanbul begin with the number 34.
   Translation: For every plate, if it is from Istanbul, then it begins with 34.

   This is the contrapositive of the first premise and might be redundant. We'll see.

5. If a license plate begins with 35, then it does not begin with 34.

Conclusion: Joe's license plate begins with 34.

- A type for plates (or perhaps just use a type for plate identifiers and then have propositions about them)

We are given several premises and need to prove that Joe's plate begins with 34.

 Premises:
 1. All Istanbul plates begin with 34: ∀ p, IstanbulPlate p → BeginsWith34 p
 2. Plates that do not begin with 34 are not from Istanbul: ∀ p, ¬BeginsWith34 p → ¬IstanbulPlate p
 3. Joe's plate is from Istanbul: IstanbulPlate joePlate
 4. Tom's plate begins with 35: BeginsWith35 tomPlate

 Conclusion: Joe's plate begins with 34.

 We are to prove: BeginsWith34 joePlate

 We can use the first premise and the fact that Joe's plate is from Istanbul to directly conclude.

 Specifically:
   all_istanbul_begin_with_34 : ∀ p, IstanbulPlate p → BeginsWith34 p
   joe_from_istanbul : IstanbulPlate joePlate

 Therefore, we can apply `all_istanbul_begin_with_34` to `joePlate` and `joe_from_istanbul` to get the proof.

 The other premises (about Tom and the property of beginning with 35) are not necessary for this conclusion.

 So the proof term is: `all_istanbul_begin_with_34 joePlate joe_from_istanbul`
