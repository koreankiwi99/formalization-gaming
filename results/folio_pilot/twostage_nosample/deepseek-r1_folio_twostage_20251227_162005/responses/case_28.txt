We are given several premises and need to prove that Marvin is not human and not from Mars.

Let's analyze the premises:

P1: ∀ (x : U), Alien x → Extraterrestrial x
P2: ∀ (x : U), FromMars x → Alien x
P3: ∀ (x : U), Extraterrestrial x → ¬ Human x
P4: ∀ (x : U), HighlyIntelligent x ∧ FromEarth x → Human x
P5: HighlyIntelligent Marvin
P6: (FromEarth Marvin ∧ FromMars Marvin) ∨ (¬ FromEarth Marvin ∧ ¬ FromMars Marvin)
P7: ¬ FromEarth Marvin → Extraterrestrial Marvin

We want to prove: ¬ Human Marvin ∧ ¬ FromMars Marvin.

We can break the goal into two parts: proving ¬ Human Marvin and ¬ FromMars Marvin.

First, note that P6 says that Marvin is either both from Earth and from Mars (which is impossible since they are distinct) or neither. However, we don't have an explicit contradiction for being both, but we might derive one.

Alternatively, we can consider the two cases in P6.

Case 1: FromEarth Marvin ∧ FromMars Marvin.
  In this case, we have FromEarth Marvin and FromMars Marvin.
  From P2 and FromMars Marvin, we get Alien Marvin.
  Then P1 gives Extraterrestrial Marvin.
  Then P3 says that Extraterrestrial Marvin implies ¬ Human Marvin.

  Also, from FromEarth Marvin and P5 (HighlyIntelligent Marvin), we have HighlyIntelligent Marvin ∧ FromEarth Marvin.
  Then by P4, we get Human Marvin.

  So we have Human Marvin and ¬ Human Marvin. Contradiction.

Case 2: ¬ FromEarth Marvin ∧ ¬ FromMars Marvin.
  In this case, we have ¬ FromEarth Marvin and ¬ FromMars Marvin.
  From P7 and ¬ FromEarth Marvin, we get Extraterrestrial Marvin.
  Then by P3, we get ¬ Human Marvin.

  And we also have ¬ FromMars Marvin (directly).

  So in this case, we have ¬ Human Marvin and ¬ FromMars Marvin.

Therefore, in the first case we get a contradiction, meaning that the first case is impossible. Hence, only the second case holds, and we get the desired conclusion.
